PRIME AREAS

Palm Jumeirah

Positioning: a symbol of luxury, beach life, brand residences and unique views.
Demand: stable, dominated by foreign buyers and ultra-high-net-worth investors.
Average bill:

  • Apartments — from AED 3 million,
  • Penthouses — AED 15-60 million,
  • Villas — AED 20–150 million.
    Payback:
  • Long-let 5–6% per annum,
  • Short-let to 8-10%.
    Client profile: business owners, top managers, high-income families.
    Features:
  • limited new offer,
  • high service charges (20–35 AED/ft²),
  • special access and parking procedures.
    Show first:
    Royal Atlantis Residences, One Palm (Omniyat), Serenia, Six Senses, The 8, Palm Tower.
    Tip: Focus on privacy, brand, and status — ROI is secondary here.

Downtown Dubai

Positioning: "heart of the city", Burj Khalifa, Dubai Mall, urban style.
Demand: Active from investors looking for liquidity and rentals and from professionals living in the center.
Average bill:
1BR — AED 1.8–2.5 million,2BR — AED 3–5 million.
Payback: 5–6% long-term, up to 9% short-term.
Client profile: young professionals, business travelers, premium tenants.
Features:

  • high service charge,
  • low vacancy-rate,
  • The view lines of the fountain are always at a premium.
    Show first:
    BLVD Heights, Opera Grand, Address Downtown, Burj Vista, The Residences.
    Tip: sell lifestyle + investment potential of the rental.

Dubai Marina

Positioning: yachts, promenade, tourism, rental.
Demand: stable all year round.
Average bill:
1BR — AED 1.3–2 million,2BR — AED 2.2–3.5 million.
Payback: 6–8% for short-let, 5–6% for long-let.
Client profile: young professionals, short-term tenants, investor-landlords.
Features:

  • high flow of tenants,
  • It is necessary to filter the quality of buildings.
    Show first:
    Emaar 6 Towers, Marina Gate, Park Island, 52/42, Silverene, Cayan Tower.
    Tip: Showing apartments with a view of the water is a decisive factor.

Bluewaters Island

Positioning: ultra-premium island near JBR, overlooking Ain Dubai.
Demand: HNWI, collectible assets.
Average bill: AED 6–25 million.
Payback: 4–5% (luxury long-let).
Client profile: wealthy families, buyers of a "second home".
Features:

  • separate access,
  • limited offer,
  • High ticket and service charge.
    Show first: Bluewaters Residences, Caesars, Address JBR.
    Tip: Sell uniqueness and secrecy, not price per foot.

INVESTMENT AREAS

Business Bay

Positioning: business center, bridge between Downtown and Al Quoz.
Demand: strong rental and resale flow.
Average bill:
studios — AED 900k–1.3 million,1BR — AED 1.4–2 million.
Payback: 7–8% (with proper management).
Client profile: "income + liquidity" investors.
Features: heterogeneous quality of buildings, it is important to choose developers of the Select/DAMAC/Binghatti level.
Showing: Millennium Binghatti, Merano/Vera, Paramount, Canal Heights, The Pad.
Tip: choose a view of the canal - this adds 5-10% to the rental rate.

Jumeirah Village Circle (JVC)

Positioning: massive investment district, strong rentals.
Average bill: studios — AED 600k — 900k, 1BR — AED 900k — AED 1.4 million.
Payback: 7-9%, record vacancy rates.
Client profile: landlord investors, young tenant professionals.
Features: Developer quality variation; filter.
Showing: Ellington Belgravia, Binghatti Onyx, Luma 21, Five JVC.
Tip: Sell as a "rental car" – fast cash flow.

Arjan

Positioning: new available location near Miracle Garden.
Average bill: studios — AED 550k — 900k, 1BR — AED 850k — AED 1.3 million.
Payback: 8–10%.
Client profile: young families, entry-level investors.
Features: construction around, but rapidly developing infrastructure.
Show: Vincitore Volare, Marquis Signature, Joya Dorado.
Tip: show  videos of the environment and development plans to help close objections.

Al Furjan

Positioning: family district with metro (Expo Line).
Average bill:
apartments — AED 1–2 million, townhouses — AED 2.5–4 million.
Payback: 6–7%.
Client profile: families and users, tenants of the middle segment.
Features: quiet area, developed infrastructure.
Show: Azizi Pearl, Starz, Al Furjan Villas, PG One.
Tip: focus on the metro and schools - decisive arguments.

Mohammed Bin Rashid City (MBR City) / Meydan

Positioning: modern center with parks, lagoons, premium class.
Average bill: 1BR — AED 1.5–2.2 million, villas/townhouses — AED 3–8 million.
Payback: 6-7%, plus capital growth potential.
Client profile: upper-middle families and investors.
Highlights: proximity to Downtown/Dubai Hills, quality foundation.
Show: Sobha Hartland I/II, District One, Azizi Riviera.
Tip: sell lifestyle: "center, park, schools, lagoon - no fuss Downtown".

DEVELOPING AREAS

Dubai South / Expo City

Positioning: the  "new south" of Al Maktoum Airport and the Expo area.
Average bill: 0.9–1.8 million AED.
Payback: 7–9%.
Client profile: early-cycle investors, logistics and aviation specialists.
Features: active construction, transport projects, Free Zone.
Show: Expo Valley, Expo City, Emaar South (Golf Views, Greenway).
Tip: highlight the  development of the airport and the future demand from expats.

Tilal Al Ghaf

Positioning: A family-friendly premium cluster around the lagoon, Majid Al Futtaim.
Average bill: AED 4-12 million.
Payback: 5-6%, but strong capital-gain.
Client profile: wealthy families, end-users.
Features: closed communities, green concept.
Show: Aura, Harmony, Alaya.
Tip: Sell "resort life" and MAF quality assurance.

Damac Lagoons

Positioning: townhouses/villas with artificial lagoons and country themes.
Average bill: AED 2.3–4.5 million.
Payback: 6-8%, capital growth up to 20% to handover.
Client profile: families, investors at the construction stage.
Features: far from the center, but a bright lifestyle product.
Show: Malta, Venice, Santorini, Morocco clusters.
Tip: explain the potential of the upside — demand is growing faster than infrastructure.

Palm Jebel Ali (New Launch 2023-2024)

Positioning: the second "Palma" by Nakheel, a megaproject of 110 km of coast.
Average bill: AED 18–50+ million (villas/penthouses).
Payback: long-term, emphasis on capitalization.
Client profile: HNWI, investors-collectors.
Features: early stage, developer exclusives.
Show: Fronds A-H lines, Nakheel Signature Villas, Beach Collection.
Tip: the emphasis on rarity and scale is the "second wave of the luxury coast".

Dubai Islands (formerly. Deira Islands)

Positioning: a coastal cluster near the old city, a new wave of tourism and housing.
Average bill: AED 1.8–4 million.
Payback: the forecast is 7–8% for infrastructure development.
Client profile: medium and premium investors, short-let operators.
Highlights: Nakheel project, logistics close to Deira Creek.
Show: Rixos Residences, Centara, new hospitality operators.
Tip: Sell as the "new Deira seafront" – a bridge between old and new Dubai.
