10.1. Principles and Rules (what is required by law and reputation)

10.1.1. RERA/DLD Red Lines

  • Advertising only with a Trakheesi permit. Indicate the permit number and RERA ID of the broker on all media (portals, landing pages, social networks, seal).
  • Exact information: area, type of property (freehold/leasehold), price, availability of parking/storage room, rental status. No "bait-and-switch" (knowingly unavailable lots).
  • The owner's consent to the photo/video and publication. Fix in Form A/additional consent.
  • Personal Data (PDPL): Do not publish documents/licenses/passport data, do not send customer data to "open" chats.
  • Short-let is subject to DTCM (tourist license, building rules). If the HOA prohibits daily rental, we indicate this and do not promise otherwise.

10.1.2. Ethical Principles

  • The truth is more important than the clique. "Embellishment" entails returns/complaints/fines.
  • Respect for neighbors and community. Do not post photos with strangers, government documents, car numbers.
  • Checking expectations. At the entrance, record in the CRM: the client's goal, budget, deadlines, sensitivity to the construction/type/floor.

10.2. Object Withdrawal Process (72-hour Launch-procedure)

T-0 (Day 1)

  1. Sign Form A (exclusive: 60-90 days recommended).
  2. Check Title Deed/POA, service charge debts.
  3. Fix the shooting checklist, agree on access (concierge, management company).
  4. Enter Contract A in REST → send it to the owner for e-signature.
  5. Apply for a Trakheesi Permit (after approval of Contract A).
  6. Book a shoot: photo + video + 2D plan + quick video tour 60-90 sec.

T-24 (day 2)
7) Get the sources, select 12-20 key photos, approve with the owner.8) Make an object card: headline, lead paragraph, 4 bullets of values, block "district/transport/schools".9) Prepare a landing page/preview page (or a "smart" link) with a lead form/WhatsApp button.10) Publish on portals, indicating Permit No and RERA ID; enable "boost"/"featured" if the budget is budgeted.11) Boost posts on social networks (see 9.6), launch a mailing list for the tenant/customer base.

T-48 (day 3)
12) Launch test campaigns: Google Search (brand + intent), Meta (video-view → lead), remarketing.13) Set up call tracking, UTM and SLA for leads (first contact ≤5 minutes, 3 days – 7 touches).14) Conduct the first impressions/video calls, collect feedback, adjust media and price/description.

10.3. Media Package (Which Is Mandatory – Wow)

10.3.1. Photography (minimum 12, preferably 20-24)

  • Shot list:
    Exterior (façade/entrance/view), living room (2-3 angles), kitchen, bedrooms (1-2 each), bathrooms (best), balcony/view, parking/pantry, lobby/elevators, amenities (pool/hall/garden), layout (if any).
  • Rules: align  verticals, natural light, without ultra-wide "distortions", remove personal belongings/garbage, mirrors/glass – watch out for reflections.

10.3.2. Video 60-90 sec (mandatory "round")

  • Structure: intro (5 sec address/project) → main rooms → view/balcony → amenities/area → CTA ("request layout/price").
  • Guidetone: no copyrighted music, subtitles RU/EN, frames 2-3 seconds, no "drone in the rain".

10.3.3. 2D plan + 3D tour (if possible)

  • The "skeleton" layout helps to close the objections of remote customers, increases the viewing time on the card.

10.3.4. Area mini-guide

  • 6 bullets: who is suitable, how to rent/sell, transport, schools/kindergartens, average rental rate for a typical layout, "red flags".

10.4. Ad texts (copywriting), templates and tone

10.4.1. Header Formula (EN/RU)

  • [Format] + [Key "Premium"] + [Location] + [Rarity Indicator]
    Examples:
    • EN: “1BR with Full Marina View, Emaar 6 — Vacant, Keys in Hand”
    • RU: "1BR with a full view of the Marina, Emaar 6 - free, keys in hand"

10.4.2. Description Structure

  • Lead paragraph (2-3 lines): who loves this unit/project and why.
  • Top-4 bullet values: view / floor / plan / parking / renovation / schools / metro.
  • Details: areas, landmarks, HOA restrictions (pets/short-let).
  • Finances: service charge (if any), ROI assessment for the investor, terms of lease/sale.
  • CTA: "Get a PDF package: layout, payback calculation, district checklist."

10.4.3. Language and Honesty

  • You can't: "the best", "the only thing", "guaranteed income", "a second to the metro" without facts.
  • You can: "view of [landmark] without the risk of overlap by the next 5 projects", "historical occupancy in the region of X% by 1BR" (if there is a source/internal statistics).

10.5. Quality control of cards (QC-checklist)

Before publishing:

  •  Trakheesi Permit number and RERA ID in the card.
  • At least 12 photos, order: exterior → living room → kitchen → bedrooms → view → amenities.
  • A video tour and a 2D plan are attached or in "pinned materials".
  • The title according to the formula, the lead and 4 values are formatted, there is no CAPSLOCK and clickbait.
  • District card (6 bullets) and point map.
  • UTM tags, end-to-end analytics, call tracking.

After publication (48–72 hours):

  • Compare actual impressions/CTR/clicks vs median on the portal.
  • A/B test of two covers (look/interior), two leads, two CTAs.
  • Price reduction/description editing — only based on the results of the first 10-15 contacts.

10.6. Promotion Channels: How to Mix

10.6.1. Portals (Property Finder / Bayut / Dubizzle)

  • Where the rental/investment segment is "burning": JVC/JVT/Arjan/Al Furjan/Business Bay — regular "featured"/"boost" slots give results.
  • Premium/Lux: Palm, Bluewaters, Downtown/Canal — less traffic, but high quality; emphasis on video and custom landing page.

10.6.2. Social Media

  • Instagram/Reels: 15-30 sec clips by key zones/views, stories with the poll "Would you live here?".
  • TikTok (30-45 sec inspection): "before/after", "3 reasons to love...".
  • LinkedIn: for areas with office demand (Downtown/Business Bay), post-cases of renting/restarting.

10.6.3. Advertising (short plan)

  • Google Search: brand queries of the project/district + intent ("1BR to rent in ...", "buy apartment in ..."). Mini landing page with 1 CTA.
  • Meta Ads: video views → remarketing by lead form/WhatsApp.
  • Remarketing: all visitors to the landing page/video >10 sec.
  • Look-alike: based on closed deals/orders.

10.6.4. Offline/BTL

  • Neighboring buildings/communities: printed cards in bulletin boards (if the regulations allow), partnerships with cafes/services within a radius of 500-800 m.

10.7. Lead Management: SLAs, Scenarios, and Conversion

10.7.1. SLA and Funnel

  • First contact: ≤5 minutes (WhatsApp/call).
  • Touches: 7 touches in 3 days (phone/WA/e-mail), then 10 days nurture, then 60 days of auto-reminders.
  • Qualification in 60 seconds: goal (live/rent), budget/type of payment, check-in/closing date, the most important triggers (view/floor/school/metro).

10.7.2. Scripts (Briefly)

  • Cold lead from the portal:
    "Thank you for your interest in [project/unit]. It's available, I have the keys. Will it be suitable to show tonight or tomorrow morning? If you want to compare, I have two more options with a similar view/plan - I will send a PDF with the layout and an estimate of the rent/profitability."
  • Investor:
    "With your permission, I will send you two scenarios: "fast cash flow" (ready) and "lower entry/higher upside" (off-plan).     Which one do you prefer?"
  • End-user:
    "Let me clarify: is it more important for you to walk to [metro/school] or view/floor? There is an option that closes both, but the price is X% higher."

10.7.3. Control Metrics

  • Response time, share of contacts → visits, visits → offers, offers, → deals.
  • For rent: "time until check-in", for sale: "time until Form F/SPA".

10.8. Pricing and repackaging (14-day cycle)

  • Day 0: Publishing.
  • Day 7: Analysis: CTR/Leads/Impressions/Impressions; if CTR is low, change the cover and the first 150 characters of the description.
  • Day 14: if there are no views/offers, either -1.5-3% of the price, or strengthening the media package (video/3D/new photos), or changing the target audience (invest/end-user accent).
  • Day 21: re-lunch: new final photo, "updated" in the title, short Reels/TikTok, mailing to the database.
  • Day 30–45: if it doesn't fly, we change our strategy: we temporarily rent it out (for sale later) or collaborate with a partner agent (Form I, "split").

10.9. Specificity by segments

10.9.1. Investment Mass (JVC/Arjan/Al Furjan/Business Bay)

  • More leads → more noise. You need short videos, clear ROI estimates, quick impressions today/tomorrow.
  • Advertising: Search + remarketing; On portals, it is "featured" in the first 72 hours.

10.9.2. Premium/Lux (Palm/Bluewaters/Downtown Channel)

  • Fewer leads, but higher value. Media – premium: interior + lifestyle, view, sunrise/sunset.
  • Often "closed" viewings by invitation, partly without public portals, emphasis on networking and direct mailings.

10.9.3. Family Communities (Dubai Hills/Ranches/MBR/Meydan)

  • Emphasis on schools/parks/community clubs, travel time, combinatorics of plans (taun vs apart).
  • Family leads are "longer": nurturing (a series of letters, micro-guides to schools and services).

10.11. Marketing for owners (listing-generation)

  • Catalogues: monthly information cards for 5-7 districts (prices/exposure time/rental market) → sent to owners + communities.
  • Yard campaigns: neat letters to the mailboxes/e-mails of the owners (if local rules allow) with the offer of "assessment in 24 hours".
  • "Neighborly" viewings: after the sale/lease – a case "how we closed" with facts (term/price/questions).
  • "Sell with us" page: video "how we sell", preparation checklist, media package, communication SLA.

10.12. Risk and Claims Management

  • Inconsistencies in the facts in the ad → corrected within 24 hours, notify the client in writing, apologize, update on all sites.
  • A complaint about intrusiveness → in CRM to prohibit autocalls/auto-mailings to this contact, to keep the proof of "opt-out".
  • Miss expectations → send a "post-visit" email: honestly about the pros/cons, 2 alternatives and the "next step".

10.13. Control sheets (for printing)

A. Start-up of the facility in 72 hours

  • Form A, Title Check/POA, Accesses
  • Contract A in REST, Permit Trakheesi
  • 20+ photos, 60-90 sec video, 2D plan
  • District Card (6 bullets)
  • Portals/landing page/social networks/newsletters
  • Search/Meta/Remarketing + Tracking
  • SLA: First Contact ≤5 minutes

B. Ad Quality

  • Headline by formula, lead paragraph
  • 4 bullet values
  • Finances/Constraints Honestly
  • Permit No, RERA ID, map/view
  • UTM/call tracking

C. 14-day review

  • CTR and Leads vs Median
  • A/B Covers & Leads
  • Price/description correction
  • Re-lunch/mailings/channel rotation

10.14. Chapter Summary

  • Legality (Permit/Truth/PDPL) + Speed (72-hour launch) + Media Quality = Marketing Base.
  • Texts without clickbait, with a clear "for whom and why" → better conversion and fewer conflicts.
  • All mechanics should be "under the ruler": templates, checklists, SLAs and a repackaging cycle of 14/21/30 days.
  • Successful marketing is not about "post and wait", but about the constant optimization of media, channels, price, and packaging for the target audience.
