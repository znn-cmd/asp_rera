7.1. Land Registration Law – Law No7/2006 (Register of Rights and Freehold)

What regulates (in fact):

  • It approved the system of state registration of rights to real estate in Dubai and opened  the freehold market  for foreigners in designated zones. This is the "birth" of the modern ownership market for non-residents.
  • The DLD (Dubai Land Department) is enshrined as the highest accounting authority for all "real estate transactions" (sales, mortgages, long-term leases, title restrictions, zoning, inheritance). The exception is  the DIFC with its register and courts.
  • Basic fees for the transfer of rights: 4% of the price (registered as DLD transfer fee), + title issue, + service fee; for off-plan – Oqood activation.

What a beginner should do:

  • Before any action, make sure that the object/right  is actually registered or is subject to registration in the required register (Land Register — ready, Interim/Oqood — off-plan). Check the status of the zone (Designated Area/not Designated).
  • In the calculation of the transaction, always include 4% DLD + service fees/title deed.

Common mistakes:

  • Sales/marketing without checking the status of the registry or attribution to the DIFC.
  • Incorrect cost planning (ignoring 4% and related fees).

Quick checklist: register type? ownership area? calculation 4%? title/encumbrances? (see also 7.4 on mortgages).

7.2. Interim Register – Law No13/2008 (+ Law No9/2009 / Decree No6/2010) – off-plan

What regulates (in fact):

  • Each sale of an off-plan unit must be registered in the Interim Real Estate Register (Oqood), as well as an off-plan mortgage.
  • The DLD can charge a part/all of the "four percent" fee already at the Interim stage; it will then be credited at the final registration in the Land Register. It is important to check with the DLD in advance about the actual FIA requirement.
  • The developer is not entitled to charge percentage "commissions" on the resale/alienation price; an administrative fee (limited) is allowed. This is expressly enshrined in Article 7 of Law No13/2008, confirmed by Decree No6/2010 and the DLD Circular (example: AED 5,000 ≤ off-plan/off-plan).

What a beginner should do:

  • Make sure that  the project and the developer are registered, have a Trust Account (escrow), received NOC for marketing; keep in the dossier: Developer Certificate, Project Approval, Trust Account No., RERA NOC.
  • Agree with the developer's account manager on the resale/assignment procedure  and allowable fees for the project (and not "percentage of price").

Common mistakes:

  • Sale/advertising off-plan without registrations/escrow/NOC.
  • Laying down the "developer's commission" as a percentage is prohibited; only limited admin fees.

Quick checklist: is Oqood there? Trust Account №? NOC? Assignment rules? FIA credit of 4%?

7.3. Escrow – Law No8/2007 (escrow accounts, protection of equity holders' funds, RERA supervision)

What regulates (in fact):

  • Separate escrow accounts are required for each off-plan project; funds are debited according to the readiness and goals of the project. RERA approves the criteria for banks/trust providers and oversees escrow.
  • A limited amount of "soft expenses" (marketing/commissions) is allowed from escrow  (see the methodology and limits in the DLD/RERA guidelines — a guideline: up to 5% of project proceeds in total for marketing/commissions, etc.).

What a beginner should do:

  • When working off-plan, ask the developer for  confirmed Trust Account details  and use only them in payment instructions to the client.
  • Keep the "escrow folder" of the project in CRM: copies of the escrow agreement/confirmations, RERA/Trustee letters.

Common mistakes:

  • The client's payment does not go to the Trust Account → high risk/penalties.
  • Advertising the project before escrow registration and without NOC.

Quick Checklist: Trust Account No? Trust Bank Approved by RERA? Purpose of Funds and Soft Cost Limits?

7.4. Mortgage – Law No14/2008 (mortgage registration, base DLD rates)

What regulates (in fact):

  • It determines what can be pledged: land, unit, indivisible share, as well as the right off-plan (after registration in the required register). Exceptions: granted land , etc.
  • The mortgage is only valid when registering with the DLD; mortgagee – a licensed bank/financial institution (Central Bank of the UAE).
  • Mortgage registration fee is 0.25% of the loan amount (paid in DLD, usually at the expense of the borrower).

What a beginner should do:

  • Always check: is the client's mortgage registered? on what register (Land vs Interim)? are there any other encumbrances?
  • In the calculation of the transaction value, add 0.25% of the DLD mortgage fee.

Common mistakes:

  • Postponing the purchase without mortgage fees → "sudden" additional costs.
  • Attempt to pledge granted land (prohibited by law).

Quick checklist: type of pledge? registration register? calculation 0.25%? status granted land?

7.5. JOP – Law No6/2019 (common property, 3 categories, committees, service charges, disputes)

What regulates (in fact):

  • A new procedure for managing common property: three categories of projects (Major, Hotel, All Other), and in the "All Other" category, the common parts are managed  by a  specialized company selected by RERA;The owners' committee (up to 9 members) is appointed  by RERA, its functions are fixed (management supervision, budgets, complaints, recommendations for the replacement of the management company, etc.).
  • Each owner has a share in the common parts in proportion to the area of the unit; alienation/redevelopment of the common parts is allowed only with the written consent of the DLD/competent authorities.
  • Removed the concept of the former JOP declaration;  introduced the Bylaws complex, the Bylaws Committee and the Building Management System + Common Property Register; disputes were referred to the RDC (exclusive jurisdiction).

What a beginner should do:

  • When listing/dealing, clarify: what category the project belongs to, who the management company is, whether there is a committee and its decisions on the budget/service charge.
  • In the presentation, show the client TCO: JOP service charge is the key to payback.

Common mistakes:

  • Ignoring the role of RERA: in most projects, the management company assigns and can replace RERA (important for the quality of service/rates).
  • Sale of "common parts rights"/operation without DLD/government approvals.

Quick checklist: category? by whom is the management company appointed? service charge/budget? are there any decisions of the committee? where are disputes resolved (RDC)?

7.6. Tenancy – Law No26/2007; Decree No43/2013 (Lease, Indexation, Ejari/RDC)

What regulates (in fact):

  • Basic rental rules: contract, obligations of the parties, extension/termination, sublease, eviction, etc.; rental indexation limits in Dubai are set  by Decree No43/2013 (linked to the Rental Index).
  • Mandatory registration of the lease agreement in Ejari (operationally - DLD). Lease disputes are considered by  the RDC (Rental Dispute Centre).

What a beginner should do:

  • Check the tenant's residency status (for a fixed term) and lead the client to the mandatory registration of Ejari; for non-residents, explain the short-let option through DTCM-licensed operators.
  • In case of non-payment/violations, comply with the procedural deadlines for notification before contacting the RDC (see the 30/90 day training questions in the guide).

Common mistakes:

  • Signing a "fix" without Ejari/without a tenant visa.
  • Incorrect application of indexation without reconciliation with Decree No43/2013.

Quick checklist: residency? registration in Ejari? indexation under Decree 43/2013? where to go in case of dispute – RDC.

7.7. PDPL (Personal Data and Cross-Border Transfers) – A Practical Guide for Brokers

Note: PDPL is a layer adjacent to real estate (personal data protection). The RERA manual does not detail the PDPL norms; below is the applied minimum of actions for the broker's office in conjunction with CRM and document management.

What is it and why does the broker need it:

  • You work with passports, contacts, PoA, KYC, bank references — these are personal data. Their collection/storage/transfer (especially to another country on CRM/mail servers) must comply with PDPL/contractual guarantees.

Minimum compliance (so as not to "burn"):

  1. Data inventory: what categories of data we take from the client (passport/visa/address/income) and why (purpose).
  2. Legal grounds: client's consent (including for cross-border transfer - if CRM/cloud is outside the UAE) + contractual clauses in the brokerage agreement.
  3. Storage: access on the principle of "minimum necessary", encryption, access logging, retention period "no longer than necessary for the purpose of the transaction".
  4. Transfer to third parties: developer, bank, Trustee, DLD – only when necessary and through secure channels; Not published to public channels.
  5. Rights of the data subject: at the request of the client – to provide a copy of his data, to correct, delete (if it does not contradict the mandatory storage by law/accounting).
  6. Leakage procedure: internal regulations: who records the incident, whom we notify, how we block further leakage.
  7. In CRM: consent checkboxes (RU/EN/AR), data export log, roles/access rights, end-to-end masking of passport fields in interfaces "not for lawyers".

Consent wording template (for the client's brief):
"I agree to the processing of my personal data for the purpose of selecting/buying/renting real estate in the UAE, transferring them to necessary third parties (developer, bank, trustee, UAE government agencies) and to cross-border transfer within the Company's IT infrastructure, subject to the application of data protection measures."

7.8. Summary "pocket" checklist for a beginner

  • Completed properties: Land Register (DLD), transfer 4%, title / encumbrances, if there is a mortgage – registration and fifs 0.25%.
  • Off-plan: the project/developer is registered, Oqood, Trust Account, NOC for marketing, the developer does not have "percentage" fees (only admin up to the limit).
  • JOP: Project Category, Management Company (RERA Appointment), Ownership Committee, Service Charge/Budget, Disputes - RDC.
  • Lease: tenant residency, mandatory Ejari, indexation under Decree 43/2013, disputes – RDC.
  • PDPL/Data: consents, purpose of processing, cross-border transfer to CRM, access restriction, logging.
