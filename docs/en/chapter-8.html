8.1. The Whole Picture: How Services Are Connected

  • Dubai REST / Dubai Brokers App is a broker's working "panel": creation and signing of Contract A/B/F, status of applications (Pending Requests → Approved → My Contracts), new permists and their management. This is directly stated in the official structure of the Dubai Brokers App (sections "Create Contract A / F / B", "Pending Requests", "New Permits", "My Contracts").
  • Trakheesi is a RERA permist system. Ads and marketing can only be published after signing Contract A and obtaining an Advertising Permit through Trakheesi (or from REST).
  • Ejari - registration of lease agreements (mandatory). Without Ejari, the contract is not protected and does not fall into indexing; in the educational materials it is directly asked as "mandatory registration in the DLD's Ejari system".
  • RDC (Rental Dispute Center) is a dedicated center for rental disputes: a separate jurisdiction/appeals/in-house enforcement service, focused on speed and transparency.

8.2. Trakheesi: Permists and Listing Management

8.2.1. What is Trakheesi and when is it needed

  • This is the "gateway to marketing": after signing Contract A between the owner and the broker, you have the right to advertise the property only after obtaining a permit through Trakheesi or REST.
  • RERA's training materials emphasize that all advertising materials (including social networks) must contain the RERA Permit Number of the listing.

8.2.2. Workflow (step-by-step)

  1. Contract A in REST → the seller receives a link to approval/e-signature; after approval, Contract A is valid, and you can apply for a permist.
  2. Applying for a Permit in New Permits (via Dubai Brokers App/REST), monitoring in Pending Requests, verification in Permits.
  3. Publication – only after the issue of the issue of the permist; the number must be present everywhere.

8.2.3. Practical tips

  • Update your personal e-mail and mobile in Trakheesi — this affects the management of customer requests from REST.
  • Manage the "portfolio" of ads responsibly: do not create unnecessary things that you cannot maintain well. This is a separate ethical focus of RERA for listing agents.

8.3. Dubai REST / Dubai Brokers App: Contracts A/B/F, Statuses and Archive

8.3.1. What's inside the app (for the broker)

  • Dashboard:— Pending Requests ,— New Permits / Permits ,— Create Contract A / F / B,— My Contracts .

8.3.2. Contract A (listing) — why via REST

  • Immediately formalizes the mandate; The link to the e-signature goes to the seller; after acceptance, the contract is legally valid and gives the right to file a complaint against the permist.

8.3.3. Contract B (buyer/tenant agency) — where and how

  • Available in Dubai REST (Dubai Brokers section): the client sends a "request to buy", the broker fills out and executes Contract B.

8.3.4. Contract F (MOU for secondary)

  • Created in the application, filed into a package on NOC and to Trustee/DLD; saves time on version control and signatures (linking to party profiles in the system).

8.3.5. Must-do checklist for REST/Brokers App

  • Create A/B/F only with full data: address/unit/parking/inclusion — otherwise refusals/edits in NOC/Trustee.
  • Keep versions and statuses: Pending → Approved → My Contracts, do not delete old versions (audit trail).
  • Show RERA Smart Card from REST/DLD and work only with licensed colleagues.
  • Verify the Ownership Certificate through the Dubai Brokers App before any contract.

8.4. Ejari: training, admission and registration of rental agreements

8.4.1. Why Ejari

  • This is a mandatory registration of the lease in the DLD: without it, the contract is not protected and is not taken into account by the index/dispute system. In RERA's training materials, the correct answer  is "mandatory in the DLD's Ejari system".

8.4.2. Who registers and how

  • Registration is carried out by authorized offices/brokers (after passing the admission procedures in the system) or by the parties themselves through online services.
  • Package: data of the parties, object, term, payments, deposit + scans; the output is Ejari Certificate (it is asked by banks, RDC, etc.).

8.4.3. Who Can't Sign a "Regular" Fix Term

  • For non-residents without a valid resident visa, explain the rules and refuse to  accompany them, redirect them to the short-let segment. This is tested separately in the materials (the correct answer is "inform and refuse, redirect to the short-term").

8.4.4. Basic Errors

  • Lack of Ejari → Vulnerability in Disputes (RDC).
  • Incorrect data on the sides/object — problems with indexing and eviction.

8.5. RDC – Rental Dispute Center: When to Apply, Jurisdiction and Speed

8.5.1. Role and jurisdiction

  • A specialized rental dispute resolution center (part of the "landscape" of the DLD system). For a number of exclusion zones (for example, DIFC with its own court), the RDC  does not exercise jurisdiction, but even there Decree 43/2013 (rental increase limits) is applied when determining the index.
  • For small claims: up to AED 100,000 judgments are not appealable; the RDC has its own enforcement service, which reduces the time.

8.5.2. When to contact

  • Non-payment/delay of rent, violation of the terms and conditions, illegal increase/refusal to register Ejari, eviction after 30-day notice in case of non-payment, etc. (these cases are separately analyzed in the training questions and answers).

8.5.3. How the process is submitted and how it works

  • Online and offline channels; Smart Judge / e-services; emphasis on speed, efficiency and transparency of procedures (officially emphasized in the materials).
  • When disputes are referred to a special Committee (e.g., on cancelled drafts), cases are exempt from state duty, decisions are final, without the right of appeal, and current court cases  are referred to the Committee.

8.6. "Combo Cases": How Services Work Together (Scenarios)

Scenario A. Secondary-turnkey transaction

  1. Contract A in REST → the seller's e-signature.
  2. Trakheesi Permit → publication.
  3. Buyer under Contract B (from the Dubai Brokers section).
  4. Offer → Contract F (MOU) → NOC of the developer → Trustee/DLD.

Scenario B. Long-term lease

  1. Listing → Permit (if advertised) → checking the status of the tenant.
  2. Signing of the Unified Tenancy Contract and registration  of Ejari.
  3. Dispute/Indexation – RDC (Fast and Transparent Procedures).

8.7. Quick cheat sheets (printable)

Trakheesi / Permits

  • Contract A is signed (REST) → a Permit (New Permits) is filed.
  • Permit No in each ad/banner/post.
  • The broker's contacts at Trakheesi are up-to-date.

REST / Dubai Brokers App

  • Working sections: Pending Requests / New Permits / Permits / Create Contract A,F,B / My Contracts.
  • Verification of ownership through the App before contracts.

Ejari

  • Registration is required; without it, there are risks in RDC.
  • Non-resident without a visa – refusal and redirection to short-let.

RDC

  • Rental disputes, fast procedure, transparency/smart services.
  • Up to AED 100k - no appeal; his enforcement.

8.8. Common mistakes and how to avoid them

  1. Advertising without Trakheesi → black points/fines. Always get a Permit and put its number in all publications.
  2. Contract A outside of REST (a piece of paper in the table) → no e-validation, disputes over authority/dates; make it through the App.
  3. Ignoring "Pending → Approved → My Contracts" → confusion of versions, NOC/Trustee refusals.
  4. The absence of Ejari → a weak position in the dispute/indexation. Register contracts.
  5. Misunderstanding of jurisdiction (DIFC vs RDC) → "filed in the wrong place". Check for exceptions.

8.9. Mini-workshop (30–45 minutes)

Task 1. Create a secondary listing from scratch: Contract A → Permit → Draft Contract F; save all versions in My Contracts. (Check: Is there a Permit No on the ad layout?)
Task 2. Prepare a package on Ejari for residential rentals; form a checklist of documents and create a memo to the client about the rights/boundaries of indexation.
Task 3. Analyze the case of a lease dispute: choose where to file (RDC or exclusion), what are the deadlines/risk of appeal, and how to speed up the execution of the decision.

8.10. Chapter Summary (1 page)

  • REST/Brokers App is your trade management center (A/B/F, permists, statuses, archive).
  • Trakheesi is a mandatory permit for any advertisement, the number must be in each ad.
  • Ejari — mandatory registration of the lease (otherwise risks in disputes/indexation).
  • RDC – fast, transparent leasing jurisdiction; up to AED 100k – no appeal; in-house execution service. Exceptions: Zones with their own vessels (DIFC).
