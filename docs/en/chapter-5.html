5.1. The framework of the legal system of real estate in Dubai

The three pillars of the system are:

  1. Institutions (DLD, RERA, RDC, courts),
  2. Registers (Land Register, Interim/Oqood, Trust/Escrow),
  3. Laws and by-laws (codes, laws, decrees). In the RERA textbook, this is summarized in a single scheme: DLD with land registries; RERA; Dubai Courts; Rent Center for Disputes (RDC); key laws (on registration, brokers, rents, mortgages, escrow, JOP, etc.).

5.2. DLD — Dubai Land Department

5.2.1. History and the "pivotal" Law No7/2006

  • DLD is one of the oldest departments of the Government of Dubai, founded on January 24, 1960. with the mandate to "keep records of land/real estate and protect the rights of citizens" (decree of Sheikh Rashid bin Saeed Al Maktoum).
  • Law No. 7 of 2006 radically changed the market: it established freehold as a class of property for foreigners in designated zones and approved the DLD as the highest body for accounting for all transactions and rights to real estate in the Emirate.
  • The DLD's jurisdiction extends to all territories and titles (freehold/leasehold/inheritance/mortgage/restrictions) except for the DIFC, which has its own registry and courts.

5.2.2. What exactly does the DLD do (for a broker — a checklist)

  • Registers ownership/encumbrances (including mortgages and long-term leases).
  • Issues Title Deed, transfers  title (standard transfer fee is 4% of the price), maintains data on mortgages, zones and restrictions on use; conducts auctions on mortgages; licenses Registration Trustees and Escrow Agents.
  • Supports Ejari (after 15.09.2019, the rental registration and Rental Index functions were transferred from RERA to DLD).

5.2.3. Where foreigners can buy

Regulation No.3 of 2006 defines Designated Areas — zones where non-GCCs can be owned on a freehold basis (outside of them, only UAE/GCC citizens).

5.3. Registers and how they work

5.3.1. Land Register

For ready-made properties: registers ownership, pledges, restrictions, easements, inheritance, long-term leases, etc. (see the list of "real estate transactions").

5.3.2. Interim Real Estate Register (Oqood)

For off-plan: all provisional rights/contracts are registered in the Interim Register under Law No. 13/2008; the developer's permissible fees, the termination procedure for the buyer's default, and the special committee for the liquidation of cancelled projects (Law 21/2013) are also specified.

Practice for an off-plan broker: before advertising/selling – make sure that the developer/project is registered, the Trust Account exists, get NOC for marketing; keep copies of all confirmations (Developer Certificate, Project Approval, Trust Account No., RERA NOC).

5.3.3. Trust/Escrow Accounts (Law No.8/2007)

Mandatory escrow accounts for the project: all payments of buyers and financing are credited to a separate account of the project; expenses are credited according to the readiness schedule; marketing and broker commissions — up to 5% of the funds received; for each project — a separate account.

5.4. RERA – Real Estate Regulatory Agency (Law No4/2019 and mandate)

5.4.1. Who are they and why

RERA was established on 31.07.2007 by decree of the Ruler of Dubai as a service agency under DLD; it is a government agency with financial/administrative autonomy, whose task is to regulate the market and protect the interests of participants to maintain investment confidence.

4 areas of work: policy/strategy, regulation and registers, studies/stages, programs/projects.

5.4.2. Specific features (what the broker "sees")

  • Supervision of Trust Accounts and Account Trustees; approval of bank/provider criteria.
  • Licensing and organization: registration of brokers (individuals and agencies), developers and projects (including free-zones), software for the market, appraisers, surveyors and JOP managers (Law 6/2019).
  • Ejari and leasing: according to Law 4/2019,  the operational management of Ejari/leasing functions is transferred to the DLD, but the methodology/index is within the perimeter of the DLD (Rental Index).
  • Education and research – awareness campaigns and publication of research.

5.5. Basic industry laws (the minimum that a broker should know)

  • Law No.7/2006 – Ownership and Registration; Regulation No.3/2006 – Designated Areas.
  • Bylaw No.85/2006 – Register of Brokers: Registration Requirements/Duties/Remuneration and Discipline.
  • Law No.13/2008 (+ Law 9/2009, Decree 6/2010) – Interim/Oqood: developer's obligations, permissible fees, termination, cancelled projects (Law 21/2013 – special committee; decisions are final, without appeal).
  • Law No.8/2007 – escrow accounts (trust).
  • Law No.14/2008 – Mortgage: Collateral (Land/Unit/Share/Off-Plan Right), Exceptions (Granted Land), Definitions and Process.
  • Law No.26/2007 (+ Decree 43/2013) – Lease: Contract, Obligations, Eviction, Index/Increase Limits; mandatory registration in Ejari.
  • Law 6/2019 (Jointly Owned Property) – owners, committees, management of common parts; RERA elects a management company/committee (up to 9 members) and can change management.

5.6. Courts and Alternative Dispute Resolution

5.6.1. Dubai Courts (General Jurisdiction)

Property Division , mediation, judgments and enforcement are part of the "larger" judicial system of the Emirate. See the diagram "Dubai Court / Property Division / Mediation / Judgments & enforcement".

5.6.2. Rental Dispute Center (RDC)

Separate center for lease disputes: mandatory registration of contracts (Ejari); there is a Smart Judge App; Law 26/2007, Law 33/2008, Decree 43/2013 is in force.
Important: the  decisions of the special committee on canceled projects are final and not appealable, cases are transferred from the courts to the Committee; no court fees are charged.

5.6.3. DIFC Courts

  The DIFC has its own registry and its own judicial system, as well as separate property laws. This is an exception to the general jurisdiction of the DLD.

5.8. Operational "red flags" (and what to do)

  1. Off-plan marketing without permissions/escrow → the risk of fines/bans. Check 4 documents (Developer Certificate, Project Approval, Trust Account No., RERA NOC).
  2. Incorrect ownership mode (freehold/leasehold/DIFC) → check the status of the land/zone.
  3. Renting without Ejari or from a non-resident → illegal: explain the rules to the client, refuse the deal, redirect to the short-let (DTCM).
  4. Developer's fees for resale/transfer → be guided by the approved limits (e.g. NOC/transfer fee according to the regulations).
  5. Long post-handover/escrow expenses → consider TCO: a limit of soft costs/marketing/commissions from escrow up to 5% of  revenues.

5.9. Mini-Workshop for Brokers (15–20 minutes)

The goal: to make sure that each listing/transaction is correctly "linked" to institutions/registers.

Steps:

  1. Open the unit card in CRM → Link: register type (Land vs Oqood), escrow status/project, ownership zone (Designated Area?). (see Laws 7/2006; 13/2008; 8/2007)
  2. For rentals, check  the Ejari flow and the client's right to sign (residency).
  3. For ready-made sales, include a 4% transfer  and check encumbrances/mortgages.
  4. If it is a JOP, check the management company and the owners' committee.
  5. Record in the client's dossier where to apply in case of a dispute: RDC (lease), courts (other), Committee on canceled projects (if relevant).

5.10. Chapter Summary (one page to print)

  • DLD is a "one-stop-shop" for real estate rights: registers, titles, transfers 4%, auctions, Trustees. Law 7/2006 made the freehold market and enshrined the powers of the DLD.
  • RERA is a regulator under DLD: licenses, escrow, registers of developers/projects, JOP supervision; since 2019, the operational Ejari has been conducting DLD.
  • Registers: Land Register (ready), Interim/Oqood (off-plan), Trust Accounts (escrow for projects).
  • Courts/ADRs: Dubai Courts (property division/mediation/enforcement), RDC (lease), special Committee on cancelled projects – decisions are final.
  • Minimum compliance for the broker: checking the status of the project/escrow/NOC (off-plan), correct registration of the lease (Ejari), calculation of transfer/encumbrances, knowledge of JOP rules.
