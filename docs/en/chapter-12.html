12.1. Why Does a Broker Need All This – In Brief

  • RERA forms are the "legal backbone" of the transaction. Without valid REST/Trakheesi forms, registration may be denied; the developer will not issue a NOC; Trustee/DLD — transfer slot.
  • Transparency and protection: forms record powers, fees, subject matter, deadlines, and responsibilities.
  • Manageability: properly executed forms = fast NOC/transfer, fewer disputes and "frozen" deals.

12.3. Form A – Listing Agreement

Purpose: to give the agent the legal right to advertise the object, negotiate and accept offers. Without Form A, you cannot advertise.

12.3.1. When to sign

  • At the first meeting with the owner, before the portals and sending the ad to Trakheesi.
  • It is advisable to issue  an exclusive with a reasonable period (30-90 days).

12.3.2. Key Fields and How to Fill Them in

  1. Property details: exact address/project/block/unit/parking lots/storage rooms.
    • Error: Missing parking space number → handover conflict.
  2. Ownership & Authority: Owner's full name, passport/Emirates ID, POA (if representative) – number/date/notary.
    • Checkbox: POA without Sell/Sign Authority – Invalid.
  3. Type of mandate: Exclusive / Non-exclusive.
    • Recommend an exclusive at a price "on the market". Term: 60 days (optimal).
  4. Price & Terms: asking price, inclusion of furniture/appliances, terms of displays, minimum price (in a separate Addendum).
  5. Commission: the agent's rate (usually 2% on sale; 5% of the annual check on rent), who pays (seller/landlord).
  6. Marketing consent: the right to photos/videos, portals, micro-landing pages, newsletters, "disclosure" about short-let, if the HOA prohibits.
  7. Duration & Termination: Expiration, automatic termination if sold without an agent? (put "success fee applies if buyer introduced by agent within tail period 90 days").
  8. Signatures & Dates: signatures, office seal, broker ID/license.

12.3.3. Checklist for Form A

  • Title Deed/POA verified, copies attached.
  • The exact list of inclusions (furniture / parking / pantry) is inscribed.
  • Marketing consent/HOA restrictions are reflected.
  • Term/exclusive/commission – clear and without "gray areas".

12.3.4. Errors and Consequences

  • No Form A → no ads, risk of fines/black points.
  • "Multiplication" of prices from 5 agencies → disruption of sales, client complaints.
  • Incorrect owner/POA → disrupted NOC/transfer.

12.4. Form B – Buyer/Tenant Agency

Purpose: assigns you as a representative of the buyer/tenant, fixes the agent's commission and the framework of interaction.

7.4.1. When to sign

  • Ideally, before impressions: the client gets a "link" to you; fix the commission (if it is paid by the buyer/tenant).

12.4.2. Key Fields

  1. Client details: full name, passport/contacts, resident/visa status.
  2. Search brief: budget, goal (live/income/capitalization), districts/format.
  3. Commission: Agent's rate (if the client pays), time of payment (when signing Form F/at check-in/at check-in).
  4. Exclusivity: exclusive buyer mandate for X days – increases the client's discipline.
  5. Confidentiality: prohibiting agent bypass in direct contact with the listing agent/owner (with a "tail period" of 90 days).
  6. Signatures.

12.4.3. Bugs

  • There is no commission (or "who pays") → dispute on the transaction tunnel.
  • There is no "tail period" → the client "leaves" to another with your object.

12.5. Form I — Intermediary (Agent-to-Agent)

Purpose: rules  of work between agencies (commission section, who manages the client/listing, exchange of materials, confidentiality).

12.5.1. Practical nuances

  • Split: typically 50/50 (may vary). Write down the percentage base (of the total commission/fix).
  • Who Leads: who prepares the MOU/Form F, who is responsible for the NOC/slot at Trustee.
  • Data Use: It is forbidden to use the materials of the other party to circumvent, a penalty for violation.
  • Tail period: 90-180 days for the client to revisit the object.

12.5.2. Errors

  • Verbal agreements → then "I don't remember". Always in writing, with dates and transaction IDs in CRM.

12.6. Form F — MOU (Sale & Purchase for Secondary)

Purpose: to fix the subject and terms of the seller-buyer transaction on the ready-made market before the NOC/transfer.

12.6.1. Form F Structure (Field-by-Field)

  1. Parties: seller/buyer (name/passport/address), agents/offices (licenses).
  2. Property: full address/project/unit/parking lots/storage rooms/title deed area.
  3. Price & Payment terms: price, deposits, source of funds (mortgage/cash), payment schedule (to NOC / to Trustee).
  4. Inclusions/Exclusions: furniture, appliances, "as-is", snagging?
  5. Fees & Taxes: 4% DLD (who pays), trustee/admin, agent(s) fees and who pays them, bank charges.
  6. Conditions precedent: mortgage approval, repayment of the seller's mortgage, certificates of no debts, release of the unit.
  7. NOC & Transfer timeline: deadlines for submitting and receiving NOC, Trustee/DLD slot date.
  8. Default & Penalties: non-refundable/refundable deposits, no-show penalties/missed deadlines, force majeure.
  9. Representations & Warranties: the seller confirms the ownership/authority/absence of hidden encumbrances; the buyer confirms solvency.
  10. Signatures & Witnesses: signatures of parties and agents; dates.

12.6.2. Form F Appendices

  • Copies of passports, Title Deed, POA (if any), transcript of inclusions (inventory list), payment schedule, details of the parties.

12.6.3. Red Flags in Form F

  • The mortgage status of the seller/buyer → failure of the slot from Trustee has not been determined.
  • It is not specified "who pays 4%/admin/commission" → the dispute "on day X".
  • "Tenant Free" with no release date/penalty → handover conflict.

12.7. SPA – Sales & Purchase Agreement (with the developer)

Purpose: the main off-plan purchase and sale agreement  (sometimes for the developer's finished stock). It fixes the stages of payment, construction terms, handover conditions, rights/obligations of the parties.

12.7.1. Key sections of the SPA

  1. Definitions & Parties: developer, buyer, project, unit.
  2. Purchase Price & Payment Plan: payment stages (mobilization/stages of readiness/handover), penalty for delay.
  3. Escrow/Trust: all payments are made to the  project's Trust Account; the developer's expenses from escrow are tied to progress.
  4. Construction & Handover: Date/window handover (usually "QX YYYY ± ..."), transfer rights in case of force majeure, notice to buyer.
  5. Snagging & Defects Liability: terms for inspection/defects, warranty periods (usually 1 year for finishing/10 years for construction from the developer - the wording depends on the project).
  6. Assignment/Resale: when it is possible to transfer rights to a new buyer (after X% payment), developer fees, NOC.
  7. Service Charges & Owners Association: calculation of service charges, participation in the association of owners (JOP), short-let restrictions (if any).
  8. Default/Termination: terms  of termination for delays, deductions, refunds (including the developer's right to resell).
  9. Dispute Resolution & Governing Law: jurisdiction  (usually the courts of Dubai), settlement procedure.

12.7.2. What a broker looks at in an SPA

  • Payment plan vs. cash-flow of the client: should payments be "split" through the bank?
  • Assignment threshold: when the client will be able to sell the right (important for flipper investors).
  • Handover window: realism, management of expectations.
  • HOA/short-let: Is it possible? Are there licenses/restrictions?
  • Penalties: for late payments/for failure of handover by the developer (credit notes/discounts?).

12.7.3. Additional agreements to the SPA

  • Side Letter: fixes personalized conditions (discounts, parking, warehouse, finishing upgrades, meb packages).
  • Payment Rescheduling Letter: When the schedule changes.

12.8. Additional documents and "bridges" between forms

  • Proof of Funds / Mortgage Offer Letter – attached to the Form F/SPA if there is a mortgage.
  • Inventory List (sale/lease) – what remains in the unit; signed by both parties and attached to the Form F/lease agreement.
  • Handover & Snagging Report — upon acceptance from the developer/seller: defects, elimination deadlines, penalties for delay.
  • Addendum about the deposit for keys/remote/material values — so that small but expensive things are not "lost".

12.9. Digital Flow: REST / Trakheesi / "My Contracts"

  • Create Contract A (listing), then Create Contract B/F (on the deal), Pending Requests → Approved → My Contracts.
  • The Trakheesi Permit for advertising is → associated with listing on portals.
  • E-sign where allowed (dual factor/OTP/scan download).
  • Versioning: each form update is a new ID/timestamp, the old version is not deleted (Audit Trail).
  • PDPL & Privacy: passport data + addresses – access is limited; sending only through encrypted channels, to chats – without full numbers.

12.10. "Red flags" by forms (and how to neutralize them)

  1. Form A without the right to photo/video → a complaint from the owner. Always a separate clause "Marketing Consent".
  2. Form B commission-free → "Who pays you?" – fix the rate/deadline/moment of payment.
  3. Form I orally → a dispute on the split. Only in writing, with the transaction ID.
  4. Form F without mortgage conditions → slot failure. Include "subject to finance / bank letter by (date)".
  5. SPA with a "hard" assignment → the investor will get stuck. Explain the threshold and fees before booking.
  6. There is no Inventory → disputes "what was left". Take photos/videos when signing.
  7. Incorrect details for 4%/commission → transfer from Trustee. Check in advance.

12.11. Ready-made templates (in a printed book and .docx/.pdf)

  • Form A (training layout) with prompts for each field.
  • Form B (training layout) with "Exclusivity / Commission / Tail period" blocks.
  • Form I (agent-to-agent): Split/Who Leads/Data Use/Tail/Signatures.
  • Form F (MOU): two versions - "cash buyer" and "mortgage buyer".
  • Addendum:
    • "Inventory and inclusions",
    • "Unit Release Period and Penalty",
    • "Price adjustment when not performing snagging to handover".
  • SPA checklist for the client (1 page).
  • Handover/Snagging Report (table of defects, deadlines, signatures).

In the PDF attachment, give 2-3 completed samples (with blacked out personal data) — this will dramatically speed up the beginner's learning.

12.12. Practicum

  1. Analysis of Form A: gives a "raw" case of the owner — the participant must draw up a correct Form A (exclusive 60 days, clear commission, inclusions, marketing consent, POA).
  2. Form B + offer: the participant draws up a mandate from the buyer and writes a short offer for the property (price, deadlines, conditions).
  3. Form F for a mortgage: draw up a MOU with "subject to finance", write down NOC/Trustee terms and penalties for failure.
  4. SPA reading: highlight 10 risk points in the SPA (assignment, penalty, HOA, handover) and prepare a "human translation" for the client.

12.13. Checklists (printable on tear-off pages)

A. Before Listing Publication

  • Signed Form A (exclusive/non-exclusive, term, commission).
  • Title Deed/POA checked.
  • Trakheesi Permit has been obtained.
  • Photos / videos were taken; consent to marketing.
  • Scans/versions are uploaded to the CRM.

B. Before the NOC/Trustee (secondary)

  • Signed Form F (MOU) with a full payment schedule.
  • 4%/admin/commissions, who pays, are spelled out.
  • Unit exemption (date/penalty) has been agreed.
  • Checks/transfers have been prepared.

C. Before booking (off-plan)

  • The assignment threshold/fees are explained to the client.
  • The payment plan is compatible with its cash-flow.
  • Understands HOA/short-let constraints.
  • The KYC package and token are ready.

12.14. Chapter Summary

  • Forms are not bureaucracy, but a risk management tool.
  • Form A gives the right to advertise and negotiate; Form B protects the agent with the buyer; Form I - civilizes split; Form F is a "transition bridge" to the transfer; SPA is the "constitution" of a deal with a developer.
  • Errors in forms almost always turn into delays/penalties/disputes. Discipline in documents saves weeks and saves commissions.
