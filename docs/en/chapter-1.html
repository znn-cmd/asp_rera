1.1 The role of the broker in the Dubai market system

A Real Estate Broker is a licensed intermediary who ensures a legal and financially secure transaction between the property owner and the buyer or tenant.

The main mission of the broker is to organize a transparent movement of objects between owners and buyers, reducing the risk of errors and fraud in the market, which annually makes tens of thousands of transactions.

A broker in Dubai is not just a "seller of apartments", it is a combination of three roles:

  1. Analyst and market expert. He understands where the real prices are, how demand moves and who buys.
  2. Communicator and negotiator. He knows how to build trust and identify the real motives of the client.
  3. Process Manager. He conducts the transaction from the first call to the signing of the contract in the DLD, keeping track of the documents and deadlines.

1.2 How the Market Works and Why You Need a Broker

The Dubai market operates according to a mixed model - developed digital platforms (portals, CRM, Trakheesi) are combined with the human factor of trust and local connections.

  • About 70% of transactions in the primary market are made through agents, and not directly with the developer.
  • More than 80% of secondary properties are sold with the participation of brokers, as only they have access to Trakheesi and can legally advertise the property.

Without a broker, neither the seller nor the buyer will be able to execute a transaction in DLD systems, making this profession a key element of the market's infrastructure.

1.4 How to start working in an agency

At the initial stage, the agent receives:

  • a cold database of leads (requests from portals, forms, or old CRM contacts);
  • a database of objects from the company (current Form A, exclusives, open listings).

The main task is to "revive" the database and turn cold contacts into active customers, while increasing their ownership base.

1.5 Basic Responsibilities of a Beginner in the First 90 Days

  1. Analyze the market.
    See prices, new projects, trends by neighborhood on a daily basis.
  2. Update and validate objects.
    Check with the owners for relevance, request documents, check Trakheesi.
  3. Work with leads.
    Make calls, maintain CRM, schedule meetings, comply with the SLA on responses ≤ 10 minutes.
  4. Conduct viewings and close trades.
    Preparation of MOU, Form B, negotiation of conditions.
  5. Develop a personal brand.
    Update WhatsApp Business, LinkedIn, Instagram, work with video content on objects.

1.8 Combining roles is the way to efficiency

The "one broker – everything is under control" model is the most viable in modern Dubai.

Why it works:

  1. Full transparency. You know each object, its prices, history, motivation of the owner.
  2. Flexibility in pricing. You can quickly respond to the market without waiting for permissions.
  3. Save time. There is no need to divide the commission between the listing agent and the buyer agent.
  4. Reputation growth. You become a "personal consultant" and not part of a chain.

1.10 Chapter Summary

A broker in Dubai is an entrepreneur within a regulated market.To become successful, you need to master two parallel planes at once:

  • Legal (RERA, DLD, Form A/B/I, Trakheesi);
  • Commercial (customers, objects, sales, marketing).

Combining roles — collecting and listing objects, selling them on your own, developing a personal portfolio and expertise in districts — is the best growth model for a newcomer in 2025.
