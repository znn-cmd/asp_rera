13.1. Portal map and their "engine"

13.1.1. Who's Who

  • Property Finder is the top in terms of high-quality customer leads (especially in premium/medium). It has TruCheck / Verified features, priority of "full" listings, advanced analytics.
  • Bayut has strong coverage for mass segments, TruValue tools/district guides, and an emphasis on completeness and relevance.
  • Dubizzle — very high total traffic, a lot of "first contact" for available segments, but demanding on moderation/duplicates.
  • Houza is a niche player, focusing on trusted agencies and inventory cleanliness; useful for visibility and SEO links.

13.1.2. General Ranking Logic (How You're Brought to the Top)

Almost everywhere there are similar ranking factors:

  1. Valid Trakheesi-permit + no violations (no "fakes"/incorrect price).
  2. Card completeness: extended fields, plans, video/virtual machine, accurate locator on the map.
  3. Media quality: ≥ 15-20 photos, correct order, hero cover, horizon.
  4. Relevance: fresh date, regular updates, quick responses to leads (SLA).
  5. Proof: TruCheck / Verified (Title Photo/NOC/Property Access) – Increases trust and CTR.
  6. Custom signals: title CTR, time on the card, saves, requests.
  7. Price in the market: getting into the corridor by area/type; "too cheap" triggers manual checking.

Conclusion: the best "SEO" for portals is a real object + strong packaging + discipline of answers.

13.2. TRK (Trakheesi) and compliance

13.2.1. Basic Rule

  • First, Contract A with the owner → then an advertising permit (Trakheesi) → then publication.
  • The permit is attached to the listing in the CMS of the portal; the number must be publicly visible in the card.

13.2.2. Portal Side Checks

  • Moderation compares: address/project/type, price range, duplicates (comparison of photos/area/location), content for "deception".
  • Non-compliance → delisting or fines in the cabinet. System Violations - Lowering the rank of the entire account.

13.3. TruCheck / Verified and the "Evidence Base"

13.3.1. What is it

  • TruCheck / Verified – Portal Verified status: The agent has provided proof that they are eligible to advertise and have access to the property.
  • Usually required: Form A, a copy of the Title Deed/NOC for marketing, a photo of the entrance/door with a number, a short video entrance, sometimes a confirmation from the developer/HOA.

13.3.2. Why Do It

  • A badge greatly increases CTR and "trust" in the card → more incoming leads, better conversion to impressions.
  • On some portals, "Verified" gives additional slots in search results and mailings.

13.4. "Perfect Listing" Architecture

13.4.1. Photographs (base)

  • Set: 20-30 images: façade/entrance, lobby, living room, kitchen, bedrooms, bathrooms, balcony/view, parking, community (pool/hall), map.
  • Order: 1 — "hero" (the best room/view), 2–5 — plan/zone of the day, 6–9 — bedrooms, 10–12 — bathrooms, 13–16 — community, 17–20 — environment.
  • Technique: tripod, 16-24 mm equi., shutter speed, verticals are even, without HDR kinks. Light - morning/golden hour.
  • Prohibition: fisheye, "microwave on the cover", visible defects/garbage, other people's watermarks.

13.4.2. Video / 3D Tour / Layout

  • 30–60 sec vertical (for Reels/Shorts shares) + 90 sec horizon (YouTube/website).
  • 3D tour (Matterport or similar) + PDF plan (if the developer has one).
  • The video raises the time on the card and highlights it in the search results.

13.4.3. Headline

  • Formula: [Type/Location] + 2 Key Benefits + (View/Floor/Optional Feature)
  • Examples:
    • «Marina | High-floor 1BR | Sea & Ain View | Vacant | Ready to Move-in»
    • «Dubai Hills | Park-facing 2BR + Maids | Corner | Keys in Hand»

13.4.4. Description (copy)

  • 1 paragraph — positioning (to whom and why).
  • 1 paragraph — product matrix: footage, plan, view, floor, parking, furnishing/white goods.
  • 1 paragraph – money: price, service charge (benchmark), rental potential (if investment), payback/cases.
  • 1 paragraph is the next step: "we have the keys / impressions today / WhatsApp + take to the button".
  • Honesty > hype: it is better to point out the minus (noise/side of the sun) and offer a counter-argument.

13.4.5. Card fields (fill in all)

  • Type of transaction, status (vacant/tenanted), type of ownership (freehold/leasehold), lease term, furniture, view from the windows, distance to the metro/schools/mall, pet policy, short-let policy (if HOA allows), Trakheesi, DEWA approximate accounts (additional plus), HOA landmarks.

13.5. Pricing, Upgrades, and A/B Tests

  • Start: hitting the upper half of the corridor by area/type (see the chapter on the market) + a window for a 1-2% decline in 10-14 days with "silence".
  • Updates: every 3-4 days — "refresh" cards (competently: do not "jump" in price, change the photo/text, add a video/tour).
  • A/B: change the cover and the first 150 characters of the  description — check the CTR in the account for 48–72 hours.
  • Lifetime: if  there are no leads/impressions for 14-21 days, the problem is in the price/media.

13.6. Anti-duplicates and inventory cleanliness

  • One object — one card on the portal from your agency.
  • Duplication (different agents of the same company with the same object) → internal fines + demotion.
  • Publish a "similar object" only if  it is really different: floor/line/view/plan.
  • For withdrawn/sold - immediately archive, otherwise portals reduce the trust.

13.7. Response Rate and Lead Conversion

  • Incoming SLA: ≤ 5 minutes (call + WhatsApp).
  • Route: call → WhatsApp card (link + "mini-dossier") → an alternative to 2-3 similar → the purpose of the show.
  • "First contact" script (briefly):
    • "Thank you for your interest in [object]. Confirm access – Are you in Dubai/online? When is it convenient to show? If it's busy, I'll send a video tour. Just in case, there are two alternatives at the same price/better look."
  • Calendar: offer two slots (today/tomorrow), address, parking/metro instructions.

13.8. How to take objects from owners from portals (listing-acquisition)

13.8.1. How to Find the Owner

  • Signs of owner-listing: no agency logo, strange photo/time format, "personal" phone, "first-person" description.
  • Monitoring key types by district: 1BR/2BR with high rates, villas with rare species — putting flags in CRM.

13.8.2. Value, not "take the object"

  • A minute before the call, collect a mini-dossier: the market price corridor, the timing of the exposition of the area, 2-3 cases of transactions, a service charge (landmark), a list of portals where the object is "sinking".
  • Pitch (example):

"I deal with your area and type of layout. Now the average exposure period for your meters is 45-60 days. If the goal is to sell faster than the market, we will do: professional shooting + TruCheck for trust + publication in PF/Bayut/Dubizzle/Houza with a priority feed. I will bring three reports: market/comparison/display plan. The commission is standard, the contract is Form A. If you want, we will withdraw it tomorrow, and in 24 hours the card will be in the top blocks."

13.8.3. Process Objections

  • "I'm already working with an agency" → "Ok, let me show you where you can strengthen it: by photo/headline/TruCheck. If there is no result in 14 days, sign A with me and I will do A/B tests and restart."
  • "I don't pay commission" → "I agree, the buyer should pay - but my task is to make the buyer pay more and faster. I'll show you the economics: 1-2% of the premium due to packaging covers the commission."
  • "The price is fixed" → "I support. Then we will add an additional assessment of visuals and exposure: we will shoot a 3D tour during the golden hour, buy boost slots at the peak of traffic."

13.8.4. Conversation Result

  • Schedule  an inspection for filming (not "meeting for the sake of meeting").
  • Submit a one-pager "How We Will Sell": Steps, Deadlines, Responsibilities, Form A + Trakheesi.
  • Record in CRM: contact, object, budget, corridor, date of shooting.

13.9. How to work if there is a buyer for someone else's listing

13.9.1. Ethics and Legality

  • We do not "intercept" the agent from the owner if our colleagues have a valid A/exclusive. We work through a listing agent/office.
  • In the request, there is  a contract B with your buyer (we fix the commission/rights).

13.9.2. Tactics

  1. Call the listing → confirm access and relevance.
  2. Ask for an agent flip card: who holds the key, how we book, what documents are ready / rented / service charge.
  3. Offer  a "buyer's package" (pre-approval/cash checks) to expedite it.
  4. Conduct  a show + two alternatives (in case of refusal/bargaining).
  5. Agree on the commission (if not the standard): who pays when. Record in writing (inter-agency agreement / email-confirmation).

13.9.3. Bargaining Scenarios

  • First, there are arguments in favor of the price (view/floor/condition/quick transfer).
  • If there is a decrease, then the escalation is step-by-step: 0.5–1.0–1.5% tied to terms/conditions (fast transfer, checks today).
  • Be sure to talk about what is included: appliances / furniture / repairs / defects.

13.10. Templates (ready to print/use)

13.10.1. Brief for shooting (listings)

  • Object: ___ (district/project/unit/floor).
  • Purpose: Sale/Rent/Premium/Mass.
  • Mandatory shots: façade, lobby, living room (2), kitchen (2), bedrooms (1-2), bathrooms (1-2), balcony/view (2-3), community (pool/hall/garden), parking, entrance.
  • Time: Morning/Golden Hour/Evening (specify).
  • Style: No HDR, no distortion, verticals are smooth, neutral processing.
  • Video: 60-90 sec, key route (entrance → living room → balcony/view → bedroom → kitchen → community).
  • 3D tour: yes/no (if yes, specify zones).
  • Extras: PDF layout, photos of documents for TruCheck (door/plate/panel).
  • Responsible/on-site contact: ______; Access: Key/Security/Owner.

13.10.2. Listing quality checklist

  • Trakheesi valid, number added, Form A in file.
  • 20+ photos, the order "hero → rooms → community → environment", horizon.
  • Video (horizontal) + short vertical; 3D tour; PDF plan.
  • Title: location + 2 benefits + feature.
  • Description: Positioning → meters/view/floor → money (SC/yield) → call-to-action.
  • Metadata: furnished, view, parking, metro, schools, pet, short-let policy.
  • The price is in the corridor, the "14 days" strategy.
  • Alternatives: 2-3 similar ones, agreed in advance.
  • SLA responses ≤ 5 minutes; The WhatsApp card is ready.
  • TruCheck/Verified submitted/received.

13.11. Contact Scripts

13.11.1. Owner by portal (cold)

"Good afternoon, my name is __ and I specialize in [neighborhood/type]. Saw your listing on [portal]. Now, according to your plan, the average sale period is 45-60 days. In order not to lose value, I offer a plan: 1) professional shooting with the right cover, 2) TruCheck — a trust badge, 3) publication in 4 portals + boosts in spades. I will bring the report "price corridor and competitive properties" and take care of  all the registration (Form A, Trakheesi). Can we come tomorrow, withdraw and start?"

13.11.2. Buyer for Your Listing

"Thank you for your interest in [object]. It is relevant; access today at [time] or tomorrow at [time]. In case it doesn't fit, I've prepared two more alternatives — the same budget, but with [view/layout]. I can send a video right now on WhatsApp, then we'll book a screening slot."

13.11.3. Buyer on a "Foreign" Listing

"I have a client with a budget __ and pre-approval. Please confirm access to [unit], what are the conditions for commission/impressions? I will send a client package (passport/contacts/proof of funds) to speed up approvals. Ready to show today/tomorrow."

13.12. KPI and Listing Manager Dashboard

  • Showcase: ≥ 95% of listings with a valid Trakheesi; ≥ 70% with 20+ photos; ≥ 50% with videos; ≥ 20% with a 3D tour; ≥ 60% with TruCheck/Verified.
  • Speed: 80% of inbound leads are processed ≤ 5 minutes; 90% are processed ≤ 15 minutes.
  • Conversion: Lead → Impression ≥ 25–35% (for hot), Impression → Offer ≥ 15–20%, Offer → Transfer ≥ 40–60% (ready) / SPA ≥ 50–70% (off-plan).
  • Discipline: 100% archive of deleted/sold in 24 hours; 100% reshooting and A/B after 14 days without leads.

13.13. Anti-Risk (Legal/Reputation)

  • We never publish without Form A and Trakheesi.
  • We do not promise "non-existent" views/features for the sake of a click.
  • We do not use other people's photos/texts.
  • For rent - Ejari and HOA/DTCM rules.
  • When working on someone else's listing, there is an inter-agency ethic (no "bypasses").

Chapter Summary

  • Portals are not a "wall of ads", but  an algorithm of trust. It "loves" verified and complete cards, quick answers and honest positioning.
  • To grow deals, the agent needs discipline: A → Trakheesi → media → Verified → SLA → A/B → CRM → updates.
  • Every day we "remove" the owners from the portals through the value and plan, and not the "cheaper commission". We lead the buyer to access + alternatives and close the offer.
