11.1. General Logic and Roles

Participants and areas of responsibility

  • Listing broker (seller/landlord side): preparation of the property and documents, Form A (sale) or Form A-Rent (lease), owner coordination, NOC/access, agreement on price/terms, transfer of the transaction to registration.
  • Buyer/tenant side: qualification of the client and funds, selection and display, offer, MOU/SPA, payment/escrow control, registration of the right/contract.
  • Office administrator: completeness check, upload to REST/Trakheesi, "Pending / Approved" status control, Trustee/DLD slots.
  • Registration Trustee / DLD office: acceptance of documents and payments, verification of title / encumbrances, transfer of rights (Land Register) or registration in the Interim Register (Oqood) off-plan, issuance of Title Deed.
  • Developer (off-plan / ready developer stock): SPA, Oqood, escrow, NOC for transfer/assignment.
  • Bank/Mortgage: Mortgage and Mortgage Registration (Law 14/2008).

Underlying Registries and Services

  • Land Register (completed properties) – title transfer, Title Deed issue, mortgage/restrictions accounting; standard transfer = ~4% DLD.
  • Interim Register / Oqood (off-plan) — registration of provisional rights and SPA for projects under construction.
  • Trust/Escrow (Law 8/2007) – project accounts of the developer, expenditure of funds upon readiness.
  • REST / Dubai Brokers App / Trakheesi — Create Contract A/B/F, «My Contracts», «Pending Requests / New Permits».
  • Ejari / RDC – registration of lease agreements and settlement of lease disputes.

11.2. Sale of "ready" (secondary market / ready-made units)

11.2.1. Roadmap (T-0 → T+10 business days)

Step 1. Listing preparation (T-0)

  • Sign Form A with the owner (exclusive/non-exclusive, term, commission, access conditions).
  • We check the Title Deed, the identity of the owner, the powers of the POA (if a representative), the absence of prohibitions/pledges (request from the owner and/or during trustee verification).
  • Get a Trakheesi Permit and create Contract A in REST → "My Contracts".

Step 2. Offer and approval (T-1... T-3)

  • We make a written Offer to Purchase and agree on the terms: price, terms, what is included (furniture/parking/storage rooms), NOC date, who pays the fees.
  • We sign a MOU (Form F) — a standard bilateral memorandum with the terms of calculations/penalties/registration terms.

Step 3. The developer's NOC (T-3... T-7)

  • The seller/buyer (through brokers) files for NOC from the developer; service charges/possible debts and NOC fee are paid.
  • The developer checks the absence of debts/violations on the JOP and issues a NOC → you can go to the Trustee/DLD.

Step 4. Transfer at Registration Trustee / DLD (T-7... T-10)

  • In the designated slot, the parties bring: passports, MOU (Form F), Form A/B, NOC, Title Deed, check(s) for 4% DLD, administrative fees, checks for commissions to agencies.
  • Trustee transfers the right to the Land Register, issues a Title Deed to the buyer; if there is a mortgage, the mortgage is registered at the same time (Law 14/2008).

Step 5. Handing over keys (T+1)

  • Acceptance certificate, meters, access cards/parking, notification to the management company (JOP).

11.2.2. Package of documents (ready)

  • Seller: Title Deed, passport/Emirates ID, Form A, NOC, service charge calculation, certified POA (if any).
  • The buyer has: passport, payment instruments (checks/bank transfers), in case of mortgage — Offer Letter of the bank.
  • Brokers: Form B, MOU (Form F), Trakheesi Permit, commission receipts, view/offer log.

Quality checklist (ready)

  • The legitimacy of the owner/POA is confirmed.
  • Service charge debts are closed (otherwise the NOC will be delayed).
  • Final financial summary: 4% DLD + trustee/admin + commissions.
  • JOP rules are passed on to the buyer (especially short-let/neighborhood regulations).

Typical mistakes and sanctions

  • Advertising without Trakheesi/incorrect information → discipline (black points/penalties).
  • Missing/incorrect Form A/B/F → risk of dispute/rejection in the DLD.
  • Ignoring JOP restrictions (parking/short-let) → complaints, NOC delay.

11.3. Sale off-plan (primary market) + assignment

11.3.1. The Framework of Law and Money

  • All rights/contracts for projects under construction are registered in the Interim Register (Oqood) on the basis of Law 13/2008; the buyer signs the SPA, and payments go to the Trust/Escrow Account of the project (Law 8/2007).
  • It is possible to advertise/sell off-plan only after verifying the registration of the developer/project and obtaining the NOC; keep the Developer Certificate / Project Approval / Trust Account No. / RERA NOC in the dossier.

11.3.2. Off-plan Roadmap (T-0 → handover)

Step 1. Booking (launch / allocation)

  • The broker books a unit, the client deposits token/booking (usually 10-20%), KYC.
  • Subscribes to SPA, registration in Oqood.

Step 2. Payment plan and escrow control

  • Payments according to the schedule (mobilization/stages of readiness); Spending with escrow is tied to progress.
  • Limiting soft expenses from escrow (marketing/commissions, etc.) to 5% of project revenues.

Step 3. Assignment (resale of rights before handover)

  • Allowed by the policy of the specific project: usually after payment of N% on the SPA; fees/penalties/new NOC will be charged.
  • The party in Oqood changes; the broker accompanies the calculations and coordination with the developer.

Step 4. Handover → Title Deed

  • When the property is ready, the developer issues a Handover Notice; the client closes the balance, receives the keys; then the finished unit is registered in the Land Register and Title Deed.

11.3.3. Package of documents (off-plan)

  • The buyer has: passport, KYC/address, confirmation of payments to escrow, SPA, Oqood.
  • At the broker: accreditation with the developer, project brief,  NOC for marketing, accompanying "investment package".

Typical mistakes and sanctions

  • Advertising/selling without  verified Trust/Escrow/NOC → risk of fines/bans.
  • Incorrect information about the conditions of the assignment → conflict and penalties.
  • Ignoring the limits of soft costs from escrow → violation of the control of funds (the responsibility of the developer; the broker must understand the limits).

6.4. Rent (residential / commercial)

6.4.1. Legal framework and institutions

  • Law 26/2007 (and updates) regulates lease relations; indexation – Decree 43/2013; all contracts are subject to registration in Ejari. Disputes – in the RDC (Rental Dispute Center).

6.4.2. Rental Roadmap (T-0 → T+3 Business Days)

Step 1. Preparation

  • Checking the right with the landlord (Title Deed/passport/POA), the readiness of the object for occupancy.
  • Sign Form A-Rent (term, commission, access, short/long-let rules).

Step 2. Offer and contract

  • We check the tenant (KYC/status).
  • We will agree on a lease agreement (usually 1 year, fix-term), deposits/checks/commissions, JOP rules (pets/parking/short-let).
  • We sign a contract (standard format), prepare a package on Ejari.

Step 3. Registration with Ejari

  • We enter the data of the parties, object, terms and payments; we get an Ejari Certificate. Without registration, the contract is not considered appropriate for the purposes of indexation/disputes.

Step 4. Transfer of the object

  • Act of acceptance, showing meters, keys/cards, registration of DEWA/gas/Internet for the tenant.

11.4.3. Package of documents (rent)

  • Landlord: Title Deed, Passport/POA, Form A-Rent.
  • The tenant has: passport, contact details, payments/deposits.
  • Brokers: lease agreement (standard), status checklist, Ejari registration.

Indexation and disputes

  • Rent increases are within the index (Decree 43/2013). Violations → RDC (there is a Smart Judge App/online services).

Typical mistakes and sanctions

  • Lack of Ejari → the contract is not protected, risks in RDC.
  • Incorrect information about JOP rules → fines/complaints from the manager/JOP.

11.5. Mortgage and pledges (briefly for the transaction)

  • Mortgages are registered in the Land / Interim Register under Law 14/2008; the subject can be land/finished unit/share, as well as off-plan rights (where permitted by law).
  • With a ready-made transaction: either the old mortgage is removed and a new one is registered, or a "transfer with mortgage" is made. All fees will be agreed upon by the bank/buyer in advance.

11.6. "Red Flags" and Discipline (That Kills Deals)

  1. Selling/advertising without permits (no Trakheesi / NOC / escrow confirmation) → black points/fines and reputational risks of the office.
  2. Incorrect indexation/lease promises → complaints to the RDC.
  3. Ignoring JOP rules (parking, short-let, use of common parts) → NOC complaints/delays.
  4. Incomplete package of documents → refusal in Trustee/DLD, transfer of the slot = extra expenses.

11.7. Field checklists

A. READY - "in DLD tomorrow"

  • Form A/B/F is signed and uploaded to REST ("My Contracts").
  • NOC received, service charges closed.
  • Checks: 4% DLD + admin + commissions ready.
  • Original passports/Title Deed/POA with you.

B. OFF-PLAN — "assignment in a week"

  • In SPA, N% has been paid, the assignment conditions  have been met.
  • NEW NOC at the developer, the fees are known.
  • Payment confirmations on Trust/Escrow are saved.
  • Updated Oqood after the transfer of rights.

C. RENT – "Move in today"

  • Contract signed, deposits/cheques received.
  • Ejari is registered, the certificate is sent to the parties.
  • Acceptance certificate: photos of meters, keys/cards, contacts of the manager.
  • The rules of the community/JOP have been communicated to the tenant.

11.8. Mini-Simulations for Beginners

  1. Ready mortgage deal: 70% LTV has been approved for the client, the unit has a valid mortgage from the seller. Make a closing plan: repayment / removal of collateral / synchronization of signatures with Trustee. (Verification of 4% DLD and Bank Claims)
  2. Off-plan assignment: the  buyer has paid 40% of the SPA and wants to sell the right. Find the assignment window in the project rules, calculate the fees and prepare a new package on Oqood.
  3. Rent and RDC: the  landlord wants to raise the rate above the index. Prepare a correct offer, calculate the allowable increase, show the client the way to the RDC in case of a dispute.

11.9. Summary (one page for printing)

  • Ready: Form A/B/F → NOC → Trustee/DLD → Title Deed. Financial block: 4% DLD, commissions, admin fees.
  • Off-plan: SPA → Oqood → payments to Trust/Escrow → (assignment is possible  according to the rules of the project) → handover → Title Deed.
  • Lease: contract  → Ejari → settlement; disputes – RDC; indexation under Decree 43/2013.
  • Quality control: correct forms and approvals (Trakheesi/NOC/REST), complete packages, compliance with JOP/ethics; violation → black points/penalties.
