4.1. How sales from developers work (quick landmark)

Stages of project launch ("lunch"):

  1. Teaser/announcement → waiting list (EOI).
  2. Pre-launch/agency briefings → presentations, price list in waves.
  3. The launch day (allocation/booking) → the issuance of quotas to agencies, the "best lines" are limited.
  4. Consolidation (booking form / token) → payment for the reservation (usually 10-20%), KYC of the client.
  5. Signing an SPA / registration of Oqood → transition to the legal plane, escrow.

What is important for a broker:

  • Be accredited by an agency with the developer and be on the priority list.
  • Prepare the client in advance: passport, address/KYC, understanding the payment plan, budget limit, plan B options.
  • Always have 3-4 alternatives (similar location/view/plan), because the "best lines" are analyzed instantly.
  • Know  the pre-handover and  developer fines/fees.

Commissions and motivation:

  • Under the off-plan, the  commission is paid by the developer (usually 3-8% gross).
  • Ready:  a  commission from the office/client + developer's bonuses (rarely) is possible.
  • It is important to understand  the gradations: base rate + volume bonus/early booking/"line".

4.2. "Developer's cards" (structured format)

For each developer, we give: positioning, key clusters/lines, standard payment plan, reliability/quality, resale policy, what the broker makes money on, what to show first, red flags.

Note: The figures of commissions/receipts are guidelines for training. Specific conditions may vary depending on the project and the time of launch. In the printed version, leave the field "Current rate on the date of printing: ___".

4.3. How a Broker Should Prepare for a Launch Day Checklist

3-7 days in advance:

  • Get official accreditation and materials (brand book, price list, plans).
  • Make  a mini-list of 5-7 clients (checked budget, goal, readiness).
  • Prepare a KYC package: passport, address, e-mail/phone, proof of funds if necessary.
  • Discuss the payment plan and additional costs (NOC/SPA/registration).
  • Assemble a "plan B": alternative lots/lines (so as not to lose the moment).

On launch day:

  • Keep in touch with the developer (account manager), understand  the waves of the price list.
  • The reservation should be made instantly; the client must be in touch (WhatsApp/Zoom).
  • Reserve 2 options at once  (if the policy allows) - choose the best one by view/floor.

After booking:

  • Send the client a checklist of payments, fix deadlines, set tasks in CRM.
  • Check the assignment conditions and penalties (write down in the client card in advance).
  • Request presentations/renders for the client, prepare  an "investment package" (see below).

4.4. Broker's "investment package" (template for 1 project, 8–10 pages)

  1. Cover: project name, developer, location, visuals.
  2. 1-page: why now (demand triggers, infrastructure, deadlines).
  3. Unit Plans: Top 2-3 plans (based on the client's goals).
  4. View/floor: line map, for which we pay a premium.
  5. Payment plan: schedule, amounts, "real cost of ownership" (TCO).
  6. Unit economics: income/expenses, rent/cap gain forecast, N scenarios.
  7. Risks/limitations: assignment, penalties, short-let/HOA rules.
  8. Next steps: list of actions, deadlines, documents, broker contact.

4.5. Red flags and "thin spots"

  • Promises of a supermarket without calculations (rent/capitalization). Demand benchmarks.
  • Too "long" post-handover without taking into account overpayment/risks of cash gaps.
  • Opaque NOC /assignment/plan change fees.
  • Weak management companies → future complaints from tenants/owners.
  • Marketing without the developer's permissions (logos/renders) → fines and bans.

4.6. Workshop: "Developer's pitch" (individual work, 60–90 minutes)

Task: prepare and conduct a 3-minute pitch on the selected project for the client-investor and separately for the family and user.

Pitch structure:

  1. Who you are and what is your value (15 sec).
  2. Location and drivers (45 sec).
  3. Product (plan/type/quality) (45 sec).
  4. Money: payment plan, income/expenses, liquidity (60 seconds).
  5. Risks and how we mitigate them (30 sec).
  6. Next steps (15 sec).

Evaluation criteria: clarity, figures, work with objections, call-to-action, readiness of documents.

4.7. Summary of the Chapter

  • Developers are the second "market" within the market: each has its own launch style, resale policy, and product quality.
  • Newbie success = accreditation + client preparation + booking speed + plan B.
  • Always calculate unit economics and show alternatives — this way you don't lose the deal if the "best lines" are gone.
  • Your task is to be a translator between the client's dream and the developer's reality: "what exactly, why this lot and how we will earn / live happily".
