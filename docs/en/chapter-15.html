15.1. Total Trade Value (TCO): What Makes Up a "Bill"

15.1.1. Purchase of a finished (secondary) object

  • The agreed price.
  • DLD Transfer Fee: 4% of the price + title release (fixed fee).
  • Trustee Fee (registration with the Registration Trustee): benchmark ~ AED 4,000–6,000 (according to the price list of the office/type of transaction).
  • Developer's NOC: usually AED 500 – 5,000 (depending on the project).
  • Commission to the agency (buyer side): as a rule, 2% + VAT (may differ by agreement/segment).
  • Mortgage payments (if any):
    • DLD Mortgage Registration: 0.25% of the loan amount + admin fee,
    • Bank valuation : ~ AED 2,500–3,500,
    • Bank processing fee : usually 0.5-1% of the loan amount.
  • Utility deposits: DEWA/telecom/HOA – often AED 2,000-5,000 in total.
  • Reissues, keys/cards: project (AED 250-1,000+).

A quick way to explain to the client: "Price × 1.08-1.10" (if mortgage) or "Price × 1.06-1.08" (cash), plus one-time transaction fees.

15.1.2. Buying off-plan

  • Booking / SPA schedule: 10-20% at the start + installments to handover (70/30, 80/20, etc.).
  • Oqood (Interim Register): off-plan registration of the right (often charged as part of the 4% already at an early stage and then credited at the final registration).
  • Admin / NOC from the developer: fixed fees (assignment, name reissue, etc.).
  • Agency commission: paid by the developer,  the buyer is usually not charged a commission for the purchase of an off-plan.
  • Trust/Escrow: all payments are only to the project's escrow account (no cash/personal IBANs).

15.1.3. VAT (5% VAT) – what you need to know

  • Agency commissions and brokerage services are subject to 5% VAT (invoice from the company).
  • Residential real estate:
    • First delivery of new housing (within 3 years of completion) – usually 0% ( zero-rated);
    • Further sales/rental of residential – generally exempt .
  • Commercial real estate (sale/rent) – usually 5% VAT.
  • You always tell the client that his payment to the agency = "commission + 5% VAT".

15.2. Mortgage in a Nutshell (For a Non-Banker Broker)

  • Registration of a mortgage in the DLD is mandatory. DLD fee is 0.25% of the loan amount + administration fee.
  • Loan-to-Value (LTV): banks in the UAE usually lend up to 75-80% (higher for residents and primary housing; more conservative for non-residents).
  • Rates: fixed/floating; frequent products – 1-3 years fixed, then variable (EIBOR + margin).
  • Additional costs: assessment, processing fee, life/property insurance (often mandatory).
  • Practical advice: Always ask the bank for  the Total Cost of Credit for the entire term (APR), not just the "billboard rate".

15.3. Escrow and Payment Schedules (Off-Plan)

  • Escrow (Trust Account) is a project account controlled by RERA. Only project expenses are financed from it  as soon as they are ready.
  • Payment Plans:
    • Classic: 70/30, 80/20 (main before handover, balance - when you receive keys).
    • Post-handover: 60/40, 50/50 (part after the keys, 1-3 years). Check for overpayment in case of stretched schedules (effective price).
    • Construction-linked: Payment percentages are tied to the progress of construction.
  • For flip (assignment), find out: from what percentage of payments  the assignment is allowed and what  is the fixed fee (the percentage  is not allowed - a limited admin is taken  according to the regulations).

15.4. How to Calculate Yield / ROI / IRR

15.4.1. Basic Formulas

  • Gross Yield (year):

Gross Yield=Annual RentPurchase Price×100%\text{Gross Yield} = \frac{\text{Annual Rent}}{\text{Purchase Price}} \times 100\%Gross Yield=Purchase PriceAnnual Rent×100%

  • Net Yield :

Net Yield=Annual Rent−Annual ExpensesPurchase Price + Transaction Costs×100%\text{Net Yield} = \frac{\text{Annual Lease} - \text{Annual Expenses}}{\text{Purchase Price + Transaction Costs}} \times 100\%Net Yield=Purchase Price + Transaction CostsAnnual Rent−Annual Expenses×100%

Where are the annual costs: HOA, insurance, maintenance, management, vacancy, agency fees, taxes in the client's country of tax residence (if applicable).

  • ROI (Return on Investment) (cash-on-cash, year):

ROI=Net cash flow for the yearInvested equity×100%\text{ROI} = \frac{\text{Net cash flow for the year}}{\text{Invested equity}} \times 100\%ROI=Invested equityNet cash flow for the year×100%

For mortgage transactions, we take into account: down payment + everything "out of pocket" (assessment, fees, insurance) in the denominator; in the numerator – net flow after loan payments.

  • IRR (Internal Rate of Return) is a discount rate at which NPV = 0 for all cash flows (purchase → lease/expense by year → sale). IRR takes into account  the time and schedule of money, not just the annual photo.

15.4.2. What to include in "annual expenses"

  • HOA / Service charges: AED/ft² × area.
  • Delivery management: 5–10% (long-let), 15–25% (short-let).
  • Vacancy: 5–10% (downtime/tenant change).
  • Insurance/minor repairs: 0.5–1.0% of the price.
  • DEWA/Internet (if short-let): in fact.
  • Taxes in the client's home country (if applicable) – advisory (the broker does not give tax recommendations, but warns).

15.6. Workshop: 3 ROI Models

Model 1. Off-plan (flip before handover or in the first year after)

Input (example):

  • Launch: 1BR, start AED 1,800,000; 80/20 plan, schedule to keys — 24 months
  • Payments before handover: AED 1,440,000 (80%).
  • Expected cap-gain to handover: +15% (scenario range +10...+20%).
  • Assignment fee (if up to keys): AED 5,000–10,000 fix (according to the project policy).
  • Transactional: at the final sale, 4% DLD from the buyer (if he pays) or take into account the discount.

Cash-on-Cash (if sold on key day):

  • Revenue ≈ 1,800,000 × 1.15 = AED 2,070,000
  • Invested = 1,440,000 (excluding incidental expenses)
  • Markup to invested = 630,000 / 1,440,000 = 43.8% (over 24 months)
  • Annual equivalent (roughly): ~ 20–22% p.a. before costs.
  • IRR: depends on the payment schedule; with a uniform installment plan ~ 18-20% p.a. before costs.

Risks/Amendments:

  • Shift handover, ± market, individual fees/penalties, assignment queue.
  • Add: admin-fees, marketing/resale, discount at the auction to the buyer.

The broker's key skill is to cover in  advance with alternatives (neighboring towers/phases), to know when the  developer allows the assignment, to assemble the client's package for launch.

Model 2. Secondary (buy-to-hold, mortgage 75%)

Inputs (example Downtown 1BR):

  • Price: AED 2,400,000.
  • Rent: AED 140,000/year (market).
  • HOA (SC): AED 20/ft² × 900 ft² = AED 18,000 (estimate).
  • Management: AED 10,000 (7% of rent).
  • Vacancy/repair: AED 7,000.
  • Mortgage 75%: loan 1,800,000, rate 5.5% (conditional), annual interest ≈ 99,000, annuity — according to the schedule (for ROI, we take a net flow).
  • Client contribution (25%): 600,000 + fees (4% DLD=96,000; trustee 5,000; commission 2%+VAT~50,400; other ~10,000) ⇒  total "out of pocket" ≈,761,400.

Net cash flow (year):

  • Before credit: 140,000 − (18,000 + 10,000 + 7,000) = 105,000
  • After interest on the loan (simplified): 105,000 − 99,000 = 6,000 (at zero)
  • Cash-on-Cash ROI in the starting year: 6,000 / 761,400 = 0.8% (low – typical for prime mortgages).

But:

  • Amortization of the loan (body) increases the client's equity.
  • A +5–7% price increase  yields +120–170k equity per year (on paper); if sold in 3–5 years, the IRR could reach 8–12% (with a capital gain).

Conclusion for the client: prime locations with a mortgage are  a game of capitalization/status, and not about a strong annual cash flow.

Model 3. Rental (rental car - JVC 1BR, cash)

Input:

  • Price: AED 1,100,000.
  • Rent: AED 95,000/year.
  • HOA: AED 14,000.
  • Management: AED 7,000 (7%).
  • Vacancy/repair: AED 5,000.
  • Purchase fees (cash): 4% DLD=44,000; trustee 5,000; commission 2%+VAT≈23,100 → total ~72,100.
  • Total invested: 1,172,100.

Clean flow:

  • 95,000 − (14,000 + 7,000 + 5,000) = 69,000
  • Net Yield on Value: 69,000 / 1,172,100 = 5.9%
  • Gross Yield: 95,000 / 1,100,000 = 8,6%

Short-let scenario (if HOA allowed): Potentially +15–25% to gross rent, but higher management (20–25%) and DEWA/harvesting → total Net Yield often 6.5–7.5%.

15.7. How to present numbers to the client (and not "drown" in details)

  1. Expense map: "one-time purchase" table (4%, trustee, commission, NOC, mortgage, deposits).
  2. Unit-economics: income (rent) – expenses (HOA/management/vacancy/repair/insurance).
  3. Yield: gross vs net; ROI: cash-on-cash; IRR: on sale in 3-5 years (3 scenarios: conservative / basic / optimistic).
  4. Sensitivity: +/-5% to price and rent (show how this "moves" Net Yield/IRR).
  5. Conclusion: "This lot is about cash flow/cap-gain/status" (choose one, do not mix).

15.8. Mini Cheat Sheet for Brokers

  • DLD transfer: 4% (always pledged).
  • Mortgage: registration of 0.25% of the loan amount + fi bank.
  • Agency commission: more often 2% + VAT (check in advance, fix in A/B/F).
  • VAT 5%: on commissions and services; Not on the price of residential secondary housing.
  • HOA/SC: The key Net Yield driver → always in the lot card.
  • Off-plan: escrow only; check the assignment policy; calculate  the effective price for long post-handovers.
  • Prime = capitalization, investment districts = yield (but filter managers/quality).

15.9. Home practice (30-40 minutes)

  1. Off-plan: take a project with an 80/20 plan, break down payments by date, → calculate Cash-on-Cash when selling on keys for three scenarios (+10/+15/+20%).
  2. Secondary (75% mortgage): Collect TCO and ROI year-on-year + estimate IRR when selling in year-3 at +6% CAGR price.
  3. Rental (cash): for JVC/Arjan/Business Bay, calculate Gross vs Net Yield by changing the HOA and management rate. Conclude: which lot beats Net better.

 

16. Lease and management

16.1. Rental Architecture in Dubai (Short Guideline)

  • A lease agreement (residential/office/retail) is concluded for a fixed period (usually 12 months).
  • Ejari – mandatory registration of the contract in the DLD system; without it: problems with visa/DEWA/communication and a weak position in disputes.
  • RERA Rental Index + Decree 43/2013 – regulates indexation limits upon renewal.
  • RDC (Rental Dispute Centre) is a specialized body for landlord-tenant disputes.
  • DTCM is a short-let mode  (daily/attended rental) only through licensed operators and in buildings/communities where it is allowed by HOA/JOP rules.

16.2. Ejari: What, Why, and How

16.2.1. When registration is required

  • For all fixed lease periods of up to 10 years (residential and commercial) – Ejari registration  is mandatory.
  • Who registers: A unified RERA agreement usually states that the landlord provides registration; in fact, it is often the tenant who does this  (by agreement). The main thing is that the registration is completed and the receipt/certificate is attached to the case.

16.2.2. What You Need to Register

  • A signed lease agreement (RERA Unified Tenancy Contract or equivalent).
  • Passport/Emirates ID of the tenant, Title Deed/Oqood of the owner (or POA + Title/Oqood, if there is a representative).
  • Receipts/receipts for payment of the deposit/part of the rental (on request).
  • For subleases, written permission from the owner/HOA, if required.

16.2.3. Broker/Manager Roles

  • Check the right of the owner (Title/Oqood, POA authority).
  • Make sure that the building/community allows the declared format of delivery (long-let / short-let).
  • Agree on the check scheme (usually 1-4 checks), deposit, entry/exit dates, defect list.
  • Accompany the registration of Ejari, save the certificate in CRM, issue it to the tenants/owner.

16.3. Extension, indexation and termination

16.3.1. Renewal – Basic Rules

  • If the parties have not sent a notice of termination/modification of the terms and conditions within the terms established by the contract (usually 90 days before the expiration), the contract is extended on the same terms, except for the  price – it can be changed within the limits  of Decree 43/2013 and the Rental Index.
  • Practice: send notifications  in writing (e-mail + courier/WhatsApp + printed letter), keep delivery confirmations.

16.3.2. Indexation (Decree 43/2013)

The limit for increasing the annual rent depends on how much the current rate is lower than the market rate according to the RERA Rental Index:

Lagging the current rate behind the index

Example. The index for the apartment is AED 100,000/year. The current rate is AED 82,000 (-18% to the index). The acceptable growth is up to 5% (82,000 → a maximum of 86,100), not "up to the index".
Procedure: the landlord sends a notice of indexation no later than the period specified in the contract (typically 90 days). Without notice – the increase is not applied.

16.3.3. Termination and "break-clauses"

  • Fixed term – in principle, it is not terminated unilaterally until the end of the term, unless otherwise provided by the contract (break clause, force majeure, mutual agreement).
  • Break-clause — specify: from what month the withdrawal is allowed, the notice period (e.g., 60 days), the amount of the penalty (1-2 months of rent).
  • Early departure of the tenant without a break-clause: the parties agree on compensation/assistance in finding a replacement (substitution), otherwise there is a risk of a dispute in the RDC.
  • Force majeure / inhabitable: if the property is unfit for habitation/use (fire, serious accident) – look at the terms of the contract, documents from the police / HOA / insurer.

16.4. Eviction – Grounds and Procedures

16.4.1. Grounds "by law"

Classic grounds include (wording in contracts and RDC practice may sound different):

  1. Failure to pay rent on time (usually after notice and a "grace" period).
  2. Illegal subletting without the written consent of the owner/HOA.
  3. Violation of the rules of the building/community (noise, damage, commercial use of residential premises, etc.).
  4. Illegal changes/redevelopment without permits.
  5. Misuse (unlicensed activity, short-let where prohibited).
  6. Emergency/hazardous condition of the building recognized by the competent authorities (technical evacuation).
  7. The owner's own need (or his immediate family) or the sale of the  property – subject to a 12-month written notice (notarized/registered mail), which indicates the reason.

16.4.2. Notification Procedure

  • Non-payment: send a written request for payment (format and deadline - from the contract). If not repaid, contact the RDC.
  • 12-month grounds (own residence/sale): send a 12-month notice, comply with the form and proof of delivery. After the expiration of the period - if the tenant has not moved out, submit to the RDC.
  • In any situation, keep records: dates, letter templates, courier/postal receipts, WhatsApp/e-mail screenshots (with timestamps).

16.5. Conflict Situations: When and How to Escalate to RDC

16.5.1. What Applies to RDC Jurisdiction

  • Any landlord-tenant disputes on residential/commercial rentals in Dubai (except DIFC and some free zones with their own courts/committees).
  • Examples: Non-payment, indexation disputes, deposit, property damage, illegal sublease, eviction dispute, return of checks, violation of rules.

16.5.2. When to Go to RDC (Algorithm)

  1. We checked the contract and the legal terms of notifications (90 days – index/prolongation; 12 months – eviction of own residence/sale; "grace periods" for payment).
  2. We collected evidence: contract + Ejari, correspondence, receipts, defect list/acts, notifications, photos/videos, HOA/police/technical supervision documents.
  3. We tried to mediate (a written proposal for settlement).
  4. No progress → RDC: filing and paying the fee (a percentage of the annual rent with limits), case tracking; deadline benchmark – "quick" review (several weeks/months depending on the complexity).
  5. Smart Judge / RDC Consultations: A recommendation on chance and procedure can be obtained prior to submission.

16.5.3. Practice of Evidence

  • In RDC, the document wins: an incomplete package/lack of Ejari is a weak position.
  • Always keep original receipts or bank letters of return/non-payment; do not rely on "screenshots" from instant messengers without confirmation.

16.6. Property Management: Office Standard

16.6.1. Before Check-in

  • Inventory/defect list (with photo/video, date/time).
  • DEWA meter readings /gas/air conditioning; registration of accounts.
  • Keys/cards/parking — a register of issuance with signatures.
  • Resident Instructions: Building/HOA Rules, Garbage/Parking/Noise, Emergency Contacts, Pets Rules/Balconies/Grill.

16.6.2. During the lease

  • Payment schedule: control of checks/transfers, reminders for 7/3/1 day.
  • Incidents/requests: CRM accounting, response SLA, reporting to the owner.
  • Scheduled inspections: 1-2 times / year (under the contract) + unscheduled for applications/complaints.
  • Work certificates: with "before/after" photos, storage of invoices, limits on "small expenses" without the consent of the owner (for example, AED 1,000 ≤).

16.6.3. Move-out

  • 7-10 day pre-inspection, checklist of defects, assessment of deductions from the deposit.
  • Final DEWA/Internet accounts, account closure.
  • Act of return with signatures; renewal of the object card (readiness for the next delivery).

16.7. Special Modes: Short-let (DTCM), HOA/JOP Compatible

  • Short-let is possible only if the following is met: DTCM operator license + HOA/JOP rules  of a specific building/community.
  • If the HOA or the Building Management System comprehensively prohibits short-lets, the violation will result in fines (and this will constitute a strong position of the landlord in the RDC).
  • Insurance/fire safety/noise conditions are usually stricter than those of a long-let; Inform tenants in advance.

16.8.3. Ejari Registration Checklist

  • Signed contract (RERA Unified or equivalent)
  • Tenant's Passport/ID
  • Owner's Title/Oqood (or POA + Title/Oqood)
  • Receipts/checks (deposit/partial payment)
  • Ejari Certificate Obtained and Saved in CRM

16.8.4. Mini-checklist "Escalation in RDC"

  • Infringement/dispute falls under lease (not title/purchase and sale)
  • Notifications/deadlines met (90/12 months, grace for payment)
  • Package: contract + Ejari, notifications, checks/extracts, defect list/acts, correspondence, photo/video, HOA/police documents
  • An attempt at pre-trial settlement is recorded (e-mail/letter)
  • The fee has been calculated and the submission has been prepared

16.9. Common Beginner Mistakes (and How to Avoid Them)

  • No Ejari is a weak position in the dispute, the tenant has problems with services/visa. → Always register and keep the certificate.
  • Failure to meet notification deadlines (90 days/12 months) → indexation/eviction fail. → Maintain "notification calendars" in CRM.
  • An increase "to the index" instead of the  limits of Decree 43/2013 → a complaint to the RDC. → Calculate the percentage of lag.
  • Taking a short-let where it is prohibited → penalties/conflict with HOA, a high probability of losing in RDC. → Check the rules of the complex.
  • Weak evidence base (no receipts/notifications/photos) → weak positions. → Document everything.

16.10. Workshop (30–40 minutes)

Task 1. Indexing.
The contract is for AED 82,000; the index is AED 100,000. Generate a valid renewal notice with an acceptable increase and a response deadline (90 days before the end).

Task 2. Eviction.
The owner wants to move in himself. Prepare a 12-month notice, a calendar of dates and an action plan (what to do if the tenant has not vacated the premises).

Task 3. Package to RDC.
The tenant does not pay for 2 months and does not respond. Collect a complete package and arrange the evidence point by point (contract/Ejari/notifications/checks/correspondence). Assess the chances of recovery and terms.

Chapter Summary

  • Ejari is the "fulcrum" of any lease. Without it, you have a weak license.
  • Decree 43/2013 – indexed according to the table, not "before the market".
  • Eviction – only on grounds and in compliance with the form/terms.
  • RDC is fast and transparent, but the one with "clean" documents wins.
  • The quality  of management (SLA, defect lists, payment accounting) reduces the risk of disputes and increases customer return.

17. Work with owners: how to get consent to the sale and manage the object before the transaction

17.1. Where to Get Owners: Contact Model

17.1.1. Acquisition channels

  1. Cold Taps
    • Calling by "owner-style" ads on portals (without the agency's logo, first-person text, personal number).
    • Address letters to selected houses (Just listed / Just sold / Market update).
    • Scripts with a focus on value (see below) rather than "give an object".
  2. Referrals
    • Neighbors, concierge services, partners (banks/mortgages, lawyers, accountants), existing clients.
    • Referral program: a fixed bonus for entering the owner (publicly declared, made in writing).
  3. Portals (Property Finder, Bayut, Dubizzle, Houza)
    • Monitoring of "private" listings, systematic maintenance of cards in CRM: area, footage, price, contact, date of the last update.
    • Quick "value call" with an offer to improve the card: photo, TruCheck/Verified, correct pricing, promotion during peak traffic hours.
  4. Community/Neighbors
    • Local chats, events in the residential complex, "district" mailings (an invitation to a free assessment and a "market-report" once a quarter).
    • Mini-stand "Free Valuation Desk" in the lobby in agreement with the management company.

17.1.2. Principles of First Contact

  • Hyperlocal forensics (speak the language of a specific tower/cluster).
  • Value > commission (show how you will speed up the sale without losing price).
  • A quick next step (not "meeting for the sake of meeting", but "inspection for professional shooting").
  • Honesty (voice the risks/disadvantages of the object and how to neutralize them).

Script (cold call - short version):

"Good afternoon, my name is __, I work on [district/building]. I saw your ad. According to your layout, the average exposure period is 45-60 days. I can reduce it by: 1) professional shooting and a 3D tour, 2) TruCheck status for trust, 3) priority placement on 4 portals. Tomorrow I can drive up, withdraw and launch the card, and you will receive a report "price corridor + display plan". Commission – Standard, Contract – Form A. Ready to Discuss?"

17.2. First meeting and verification of the owner's rights

17.2.1. Meeting Documents (Minimum)

  • Title Deed (original/copy) — check the name, unit number, type of right (freehold/leasehold).
  • Emirates ID / Passport of  the owner; for a non-resident – passport.
  • POA (if there is a representative): legalization/translation, special powers (sell, sign Form A/SPA/Contract F, NOC, receipt/issuance of checks, registration with the Trustee).
  • Mortgage: bank statement/balance statement; release procedure.
  • Rental status: valid Ejari, deposit, check schedule; 12-month notices (if eviction is planned).
  • Service Charge (HOA): no debt/annual budget.

17.2.2. Inspection and "technical" checklist

  • Condition inspection (defects, view, light, acoustics, smells, parking).
  • Short-let (if invest-positioning) is allowed, HOA/JOP rules.
  • Preparation for shooting: cleaning, light, staging (minimal), time of the "golden hour".

17.2.3. Pricing and strategy

  • Corridor by area/type (from your "Area Guide").
  • Start in the upper half of the corridor + a window of 1-2% decline in the absence of traffic in the first 10-14 days.
  • A/B plan: 2 versions of the cover/title, "refresh" every 3-4 days.

17.3. Form A (Agency Agreement): Why, When, and How

17.3.1. The Role of Form A

  • This is an official agreement between the owner and the agency/broker for the marketing/sale of a specific property.
  • Without Form A, it is forbidden to advertise and request a Trakheesi permit (the permit number is attached to the listing).
  • Form A is created in the Dubai REST / Dubai Brokers App: "Create Contract A", then the card goes to My Contracts.

17.3.2. When required

  • Always, if you are going to market an object: portals, social networks, newsletters, impressions.
  • In the case of "silent selling" (off-market), it is also mandatory (indicate that advertising is limited to closed channels).

17.3.3. Form A Sections (What to Talk to the Owner)

  • Parties and object (full name/passport/ID, address, No unit, area).
  • Validity period (usually 60-90 days, extendable).
  • Type: Exclusive / Non-Exclusive.
  • Commission: amount and who pays (usually the owner, but flexible); success fee conditions.
  • Price and permissible bargaining range, deposit/deposit conditions, NOC.
  • Media rights (photo/video/3D), compliance with HOA/JOP and DTCM rules (if short-let).
  • Conflicts of interest/referral connections are best disclosed in advance.
  • Termination: grounds, notices, reimbursement of costs.
  • Data protection (PDPL minimum): consent to processing and cross-border transfer for the purposes of the transaction.

17.3.4. Exclusive / Non-Exclusive – How to Choose

  • Exclusive (recommended): single responsibility center, single price/packaging/strategy; higher chance of TruCheck/Verified and priority on portals.
  • Non-exclusive: allowed if the owner insists, but fix the photo/price rules to avoid the "fake market".

17.3.5. Binding to Trakheesi/REST (Operational Thread)

  1. In REST, create Contract A → send a link to the e-signature to the owner.
  2. After signing in My Contracts,  the status becomes active.
  3. We apply for an advertising permit (Trakheesi) → get a number.
  4. Upload the listing to the portals with the Trakheesi number + package on TruCheck/Verified.

Important: keep the signed A, confirmations, permit number, and the entire media package in the object's CRM folder (auditability).

17.4. Compliance Risks and Broker Discipline

17.4.1. Bylaw 85/2006 (broker/office registration)

  • Only a registered broker (DREI license/certificate) as part of a licensed agency (Trade License + registration in DLD/RERA) can work.
  • Annual renewal of statuses and compliance with the code of conduct: transparent contracts (A/B/F), prohibition of false advertising, work only with real objects, prohibition of "dummy" prices.

17.4.2. Red Flags and Sanctions

  • Advertising without Form A/Trakheesi → removal/fines/downgrading of the office on portals.
  • Incorrect data (address/footage/type/price) → blocking/complaints in DLD/RERA.
  • Illegitimate processing of personal data → PDPL risks (include consents in templates).
  • Bypassing colleagues with a valid exclusive → reputational damage and claims.

17.5. Interaction and disputes: where to escalate

17.5.1. What to Decide "Within the Deal" (Before Escalation)

  • Price/bargaining disagreements are recorded in the written appendices to Form A.
  • Media/Description Quality – Office Standard + Consistent Tone.
  • Impression dates/access — calendar, backup slots with a key holder.

17.5.2. Mediation

  • If the dispute is "non-judicial" (who conducts the showings, photo replacement, partial payment for boosts) – a quick mediation call (you/owner/office manager), the protocol is in the letter.
  • As for money/violations, record a "claim" with a date and deadline.

17.5.3. Where to Next

  • RDC – if the dispute is lease/JOP/service-charge/eviction.
  • DLD/RERA - Reports of false advertising/violation of marketing rules.
  • Dubai Courts – Property claims outside the lease (e.g., refusal to transfer title deeds after performance, unsettled sales obligations).

17.6. Scripts and Templates (Print-Ready)

17.6.1. One-pager "Offer to the Owner"

Goal: to take the property into an exclusive for 60-90 days.
Contents on 1 page:

  • Who we are and why your building/layout is our specialty.
  • 14-day plan: professional shooting (date), 3D tour, TruCheck, publication on 4 portals, boost slots in peak, mailing to the database + neighbors.
  • Price strategy: start in the upper half of the corridor; if there is no traffic, -1-2% on day 10-14.
  • Reporting: weekly report (views/requests/impressions/comments).
  • Contract: Form A, exclusive/non-exclusive, commission __%.
  • Timeframe: 60-90 days with the right to extend.

17.7. Office Operating Regulations (briefly)

  1. Without Form A, there's no marketing.
  2. Each object is a folder in CRM: A, Trakheesi, media, reports, correspondence.
  3. SLA reporting to the owner: weekly summary (CTR, leads, impressions, feedback, weekly plan).
  4. A/B plan: if 10-14 days are "quiet" – change of cover/text, reduction by 1-2%, addition of video/tour.
  5. Closing: Handover Act, Finringtone, Thank You, and Referral Request.

17.8. Common Mistakes (and How to Avoid Them)

  • Advertising "in words" without A/Trakheesi → risk of fines/bans on portals.
  • "General" POA instead of a special → refusal from Trustee on the transaction.
  • Unaccounted mortgage/HOA debt → disruption of the NOC/Transfer day.
  • Overpricing without a plan → the listing "burns out" in 2 weeks; always offer an A/B and a window for correction.
  • Ignore PDPL → store consents/mask sensitive fields, restrict access.

17.9. Practicum (30–40 minutes)

  1. Case "private owner on the portal". In 10 minutes, prepare a "one-pager" and a script, schedule an inspection for shooting, and collect a package of documents under Form A.
  2. Solution "exclusive/non-exclusive". Introductory information is given: the owner insists on non-exclusivity and an inflated price. Offer a compromise and fix it in A (conditions for the appearance of an exclusive in 14 days in the absence of leads).
  3. Risk audit. Check the layout of the Form A: whether there are media authorizations, PDPL consents, NOC/mortgage summary, Trakheesi binding.

Section Summary

  • Form A → Trakheesi → portals are the only way to go.
  • Examination of the area + honest packaging + reporting discipline = consent of the owner and successful sale.
  • Document everything: law, authority, NOC/mortgage, PDPL. This saves weeks and saves deals.

18. How to Sell Fast: 14-30-60 Day Listing Strategy

18.1. General Logic

Quick sale = (1) correct price in the neighborhood corridor, (2) strong packaging, (3) aggressive marketing mix, (4) processes without speed failures, (5) clean legal track. We manage in three time windows: 0-14, 15-30, 31-60 days.

18.2. Pricing: how to "get" into demand

18.2.1. District corridor

  • Assembling 5-10 real analogues: the same project/cluster, type of layout, condition, view/floor.
  • Form  a corridor (min–median–max) and a trading fork (usually 1.5–4% for lots up to $2–3 million; in prime — individually).

18.2.2. Start and Early Decline Window

  • Start: The upper half of the corridor if the object is visually/visually better than the median.
  • Downside window: if there is not enough  traffic in 10-14 days (see KPI below) — -1-2%.
  • Next: on day 28-30 — an additional adjustment of -1-1.5% or a strengthening of the packaging/media if there are leads, but do not convert.

18.2.3. Peer Benchmarks

  • We keep a live table of analogues (links to portals, cover photos, price/m², floor/view, days on sale).
  • Every week -  a screenshot of the "who withdrew/sold/decreased" to argue the price change to the owner.

Pricing KPI (first 14 days):

  • CTR of cards ≥ 3–5%;
  • Leads/day ≥ 3-5 (mass) or ≥ 1-2 (prime);
  • Impressions/week ≥ 3-6 (mass) or ≥ 2-3 (prime).

18.3. Packaging: to "want to call"

18.3.1. Media Pack (Minimum)

  • Photos 20–30: the hero-frame (cover), day area, bedrooms, bathrooms, balcony/view, community, entrance/lobby.
  • Video: 60-90 sec (horizon) + short vertical (reels).
  • 3D tour (Matterport) + PDF plan.
  • Type/floor/parking/decoration are in the first lines of the description (these are the key USPs).

18.3.2. Text and Title

  • Title: [location/type] + 2 benefits + trait ("Marina | High-floor 1BR | Sea & Ain View | Vacant»).
  • Description: Positioning → characteristics → money (SC/yield, if applicable) → next step ("impressions today/keys with us").

18.3.3. Viewing-ready checklist

  • Cleaning/ventilation, working lights, curtains open, access by key (not through 5 "approvals"), parking for the client, printed plan, mini-booklet "about the area" (schools/shops/park/roads).
  • On-site: visit sheet (name/contact/comment), 2-3 alternatives "in case of objections".

18.4. Marketing mix: from portals to remarketing

18.4.1. Portals

  • Property Finder / Bayut / Dubizzle / Houza: full profile + Trakheesi + submission to Verified/TruCheck.
  • Placement in the first 24-48 hours after shooting.
  • A/B of two covers and the first 150 characters of text – 48-72 hour test.

18.4.2. Micro-landing page (1-screen landing page)

  • Hero cover, 6-8 key photos, layout, 4 USP bullets, map/time to the metro, WhatsApp button, application form.
  • UTM tags for all sources. Pixels (Meta/Google) for remarketing.

18.4.3. Mailings and Databases

  • Database of investors/buyers: "New to Market" letter (3 photos + plan + theses + 2 alternatives).
  • Inter-agency database : short listing + commission/access conditions.

18.4.4. Target and Remarket

  • Remarket for "viewers" of the card/landing page: 3-5 creatives (hero-photo, view, layout).
  • Light target according to the interests of the area (if appropriate), but the main budget is a remarket of 7-14 days.

18.5. Processes: Speed Kills Competition

18.5.1. Response Speed

  • Incoming SLA: ≤ 10 minutes (call + WhatsApp card + 2 alternatives).
  • WA template: link to the card, plan, "access today/tomorrow", Google parking location.

18.5.2. Impression Windows

  • We immediately reserve 2 windows/day for viewings (weekdays, evening + weekend).
  • For occupied apartments — single slots with the tenant, at least 24 hours notice.

18.5.3. Weekly Report to the Owner (Skeleton of the Letter)

  • Week No__: impressions __ / leads __ / CTR __ / customer comments (3-5 theses).
  • Market/analogues: who withdrew/changed the price.
  • Next week's plan: A/B cover, boost on the portal, newsletter, price adjustment (if necessary).
  • Solutions: Align X, Y, Z.

18.5.4. A/B Tests

  • Day 1–3: Two covers (view vs interior).
  • Day 7–10: Two versions of the title (view/floor vs parking/finishing).
  • We look at the CTR/leads/time on the page → leave the winner.

18.6. Legal nuances: transaction protection and money

18.6.1. Fees and Costs

  • Record in the letter and in the contract (Form A / inter-agency / MOU):
    • who pays the commission (seller/buyer/equally),
    • amount (% + VAT 5%),
    • when it is paid (usually at the signing of MOU/Contract F),
    • who pays the NOC, Trustee fee, appraisal/mortgage fees, DLD 4% (usually the buyer).

Example wording (to the buyer):
"Buyer agrees to pay Agency Fee of 2% + VAT 5% of the purchase price upon signing MOU/Contract F. All DLD transfer fees and Trustee fees are borne by the Buyer."

18.6.2. Transaction Documents and Finalization

  • Ready market: Form A (listing), for showings — Form B with the buyer, then MOU/Contract F → NOC → Registration Trustee → Transfer → Title Deed.
  • Off-plan: SPA/Oqood → Assignment (if resale) or Handover → final registration.
  • Money: only manager's cheques / escrow / Trust Account (off-plan), no cash.

18.6.3. Working with POAs

  • It is permissible if: Special POA with the right to sell/sign Form A/B/F/MOU/NOC/receive/issue checks/be present with the Trustee/register.
  • Overseas POA: Legalization + Sworn Arabic Translation.
  • Notify the other party in advance, attach a copy of the POA to the "pre-transfer" package.

18.7. 14–30–60: Roadmap for Action

0-48 hours (pre-launch)

  1. Documents: Form A signed, Trakheesi filed.
  2. Shooting: photo/video/3D/plan - on the same day.
  3. Cards: portals + micro-landing page + UTM + pixels.
  4. Mailings: investors and agents.
  5. The incoming script is ready; the display windows are set.

Day 1–14 (First Strike)

  • A/B covers and titles.
  • Portal boosts (peak days/hours).
  • Remarket for "watchers".
  • KPIs: leads/day ≥ 3-5 (mass) / ≥ 1-2 (prime); impressions/week ≥ 3-6 / ≥ 2-3.
  • If we do not meet → audit: price/media/headline → −1-2% or media amplification (add video/tour).

Day 15–30 (second pace)

  • Update the media (new cover/plan/view from the window), the second wave of mailings.
  • Rotation on portals (refresh).
  • Open-house slot (if the format of the house allows).
  • Negotiations: collecting feedback, forming a bargaining package (escalation 0.5–1.0–1.5%).
  • If there is no offer and the metrics are weak → −1–1.5% + new positioning (for example, "better view/parking included").

Day 31–60 (third attempt or restart)

  • Repackaging: a new series of photos (evening/other light), "story-video", a case-calculator of payback (if an investment profile).
  • Reassessment: updated benchmark of analogues; make a decision - another -1-2% or withdraw/pause and restart in 2 weeks with a new strategy.
  • In prime, "waiting" is allowed if there are impressions and weak alternatives from buyers, but with weekly reports to the owner.

18.8. Checklists and templates

18.8.1. Checklist of readiness for launch

  • Form A, Trakheesi
  • 20–30 photos, videos, 3D, plan
  • Text/title by formula
  • Cards on 4 portals
  • Micro Landing Page + UTM + Pixels
  • Mailing to investors/agents
  • Remarket campaigns launched
  • Impression slots booked
  • WA template and alternative pack ready

18.8.2. Weekly Report to the Owner (Structure)

  • Statistics: impressions/leads/CTR/average time on the card
  • Customer feedback (5-7 points)
  • Market: dynamics of 5–10 analogues
  • Solution: (1) leave the price and strengthen the media; (2) reduce by X%; (3) Change Positioning
  • Weekly plan: A/B, boosts, newsletters, open-house

18.8.3. First Response Script (≤10 minutes)

"Thank you for your interest in [object]. Impressions are available today at [time] or tomorrow at [time]. I send a link with a plan and a video. In case this one does not "fall", I have prepared 2 alternatives in the same budget. Which location is more convenient for you?"

18.9. Common mistakes and how to avoid them

  • Overestimation without a plan → "burnout" of the listing in 2 weeks. Always set a window  of −1-2% for day 10-14.
  • Weak cover and first paragraph → low CTR. Do A/B.
  • Slow response → loss of hot leads. SLA ≤ 10 minutes.
  • No UTM/landing page → it's not clear what works. Mark everything.
  • Legal ambiguity (who pays what, when commission, POA) → conflicts at the finish line. Record in the letter and in the contract.
  • Low-quality impressions (difficult access, dirt, dark) → prepare the object.

18.10. Summary for the Broker

  • Price in the corridor + strong packaging + speed → 70% success.
  • Marketing mix + remarket + reporting → another 20%.
  • Legal Integrity and Discipline (Form A → Trakheesi → MOU/F → NOC → Transfer) – the remaining 10% and your insurance.
