9.1. What is POA (Power of Attorney) in Dubai Real Estate Transactions

Definition. A power of attorney (POA) is a written authorization under which the principal (owner or buyer) authorizes another person to act on his behalf within strictly defined actions (sell, buy, receive NOC, sign a contract, register a transaction, etc.).

Key principles for real estate transactions:

  • Special, not general.In practice, a special power of attorney is required for the sale/purchase/mortgage of real estate in Dubai, which clearly indicates the actions (for example: "the right to sell the property..., sign Form A/B/F, MOU/SPA, submit and receive NOC, accept/issue checks, be present at the Registration Trustee, register the transfer of rights in the DLD").
  • Localization and language. A power of attorney drawn up outside the UAE must be legalized (the country of issue → the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of the country → the UAE Embassy/Consulate) and attestation by the UAE Ministry of Foreign Affairs; the text for the DLD is in Arabic or with a sworn legal translation into Arabic (bilingual forms are welcome).
  • Object identification. For "sale", it is advisable to specify a specific object (Community/Building/Unit/Title No) or at least a type/project, so that there are no doubts about the scope of permissions.
  • Validity and revocation. POA is valid until the expiration of the period specified in the document, or until its revocation by the principal (the revocation must be officially registered and brought to the attention of counterparties), or until the loss of legal capacity/death of the principal (in such cases, it is terminated, unless otherwise expressly provided by law).
  • Restrictions. Some actions require the presence of the owner in person (rare, but occurs in some banking/legal procedures). The Registration Trustee has the right to refuse registration if there are doubts about the scope/authenticity of the POA.
  • Conflict of interest. If the proxy agent is also a commercial beneficiary, this should be transparently recorded; it is best to avoid situations where the representative sells "to himself" without a separate board resolution.

Typical types of POA in real estate practice:

  • Sell/buy is the most common.
  • Mortgage is the right to formalize/repay.
  • Lease (Ejari) – the right to sign and register contracts, renew, receive/write checks, submit to the RDC.
  • Property management is the right to deliver, service, receive payments, represent interests in HOA/JOP, with DEWA/Etisalat/du.
  • Complex ( sell/buy/lease/NOC/registration) is a "package" for non-residents.

9.2. When POA is Acceptable/Desirable/Undesirable

Acceptable/desirable:

  • The owner/buyer is a non-resident and cannot be personally present at the Trustee/DLD.
  • Sale of the inherited asset after the completion of the inheritance procedure and the appointment of the executor – the probability of POA from the executor.
  • A legal entity acts through an authorized employee, and it is more convenient to issue a one-time Special POA (instead of the general "right to sign").

Undesirable or with increased control:

  • Too broad "General POA" without specifics.
  • POA issued "a long time ago" without mentioning the object/actions (risk of refusal at the Trustee).
  • Any conflicts of interest (the representative is the second party to the transaction).
  • Countries/documents with problematic verification — check the legalization chain especially carefully.

9.3. Due Diligence (DD) of the individual seller (ready/off-plan)

Purpose: to confirm the right and authority of the seller, the absence of hidden encumbrances, the correctness of calculations, the possibility of transfer without risks.

Package and checks (ready):

  1. Title Deed (original + copy) — check the name/passport, area, title type (freehold/leasehold), number.
  2. Passport/Emirates ID – match of data with the title; visa relevance (if it affects attendance/departure).
  3. POA (if there is a representative) – legalization/translation/scope of authority; "sell/receive checks/NOC/sign Form A/SPA/Transfer" must be explicitly stated.
  4. Mortgage – bank certificate/statement: balance, closing procedure, who brings the release letter; settlement mechanism (bank/manager's checks) to be agreed upon in advance.
  5. Service charge/HOA – confirmation of the absence of debt; the presence of disputes in the JOP (committee/management company).
  6. Developer's NOC – the procedure and terms of receipt; fee amounts and who pays.
  7. Lease (if rented) – valid Ejari (term, deposit, checks on hand), eviction rights/notices (12-month, etc.), tenant's consent to viewings.
  8. Taxes/sanctions/AML screening (office procedures): sanctions lists, PEP, source of funds when reinvesting (if the bank requires).

Package and checks (off-plan):

  1. Oqood/SPA — registration of the right according to the intermediate register; reconcile the payment stage/schedule.
  2. Payment receipts – actual payments and coincidence with the SPA schedule.
  3. Assignment policy — the conditions for assigning before handover: whether the assignment is allowed, the fee/penalty formula, who submits the package.
  4. Trust Account is a buyer's payment channel strictly for project escrow.
  5. POA is a separate right of assignment/signing from the developer (many require a special wording "to sign assignment/transfer with developer").
  6. Layout changes/variations — so as not to get rejected for a handover.

Red flags of the seller: discrepancy in the data of the Title passport ↔; "general" POA without specifics; lack of Oqood/receipts; large debts on service charges; "verbal" consent of the bank instead of a formal release process.

9.4. Due Diligence of the Individual Buyer

Purpose: to confirm the legal capacity and solvency, the purity of the origin of funds (at the request of the bank/counterparties), the absence of restrictions on the transaction.

Package & Checks:

  1. Passport/visa/Emirates ID (if resident).
  2. Address/contacts – for Contract B/F, bank/manager's checks, notifications.
  3. POA (if representative is in charge) – legalization/scope of authority "buy/make payments/sign F/register/receive keys".
  4. Mortgage – pre-approval (if a mortgage purchase) or proof of funds.
  5. AML/sanctions — internal office screening; upon request — source of funds (bank letters, statements, asset sale agreements).
  6. Tax residency/compliance – if necessary (for bank/transfers).

Buyer's red flags: refusal to provide basic KYC; "third parties pay" without documents; aggressive terms "no bank/no funds".

9.5. Due Diligence of a legal entity (seller/buyer)

The purpose is to make sure that the legal entity exists, is authorized to make a transaction, and the signatory really has the right to sign.

Package & Checks:

  1. Trade License (current), if necessary, Certificate of Incorporation/Good Standing.
  2. MOA/AOA (Articles of Incorporation) – check the right to sell/buy/pledge, restrictions, quorum/signatures.
  3. Board Resolution / Shareholders Resolution is a special decision on the sale/purchase of a specific unit (or the provision of a POA for a specific representative) with the authority to "sign all documents, submit and receive NOCs, be present at the Trustee, accept/issue payments".
  4. Specimen Signatures / signatory card, passport / Emirates ID of the authorized person.
  5. UBO-structure (if required by the bank/compliance), sanctions/PEP screening.
  6. For free-zones/offshores (JAFZA/RAKICC, etc.) – legalization of documents, apostilles/consular attestation, sworn translation; sometimes – Certificate of Incumbency/Directors.
  7. POA from a legal entity (if signed by a non-director) – all requirements for POA (see 9.1), plus a link to the decision of the board/shareholders on the basis of which the POA was issued.

Red flags: "generic" board resolution without mentioning the object/actions; expired Trade License; inconsistency between the signer and the right to sign on the statutory documents; impenetrable UBO-structure (sanctions/refusals of banks).

9.6. Checklists for Purchase and Sale Transactions

9.6.1. Ready (secondary market) — a "short" broker's checklist

Pre-Listing / Contract A:

  • Title Deed (copy/verification)
  • Seller's Passport/ID
  • POA (if any): legalization, transfer, scope of authority (sell/NOC/cheques/F/transfer)
  • Rental Status: Ejari, Terms, Deposit, Notifications
  • Service Charge: Debt Certificate/HOA Budget
  • Mortgage: balance, bank, release order
  • Developer's NOC: requirements, amounts, deadlines
  • Contract A signed → Trakheesi permit received → marketing

Before Contract F/Trustee:

  • The data of the parties are finalized (passport/ID/POA/board resolution)
  • A set of payment instruments (manager's cheques) has been agreed
  • All fifs included: 4% DLD, Trustee fee, NOC, mortgage 0.25% (if any)
  • Encumbrances/pledges/courts have been checked (if possible, a request from Trustee/DLD)
  • The "day of the transaction" is scheduled (timing of NOC → Trustee → Transfer → issuance of the title)

After:

  • Received a new Buyer's Title Deed
  • Key Handover/DEWA/Etisalat/HOA
  • Brokerage file closed (commissions/acts/invoices/scans)

9.6.2. Off-plan (assignment) checklist

Before marketing:

  • SPA/Oqood in the name of the seller
  • Payment receipts (matching graphics)
  • Developer's assignment policy  (fees, admission stage)
  • Trust Account details (for buyer's payments)
  • Contract A → Advertising permit (if public marketing)

It's a deal:

  • Developer's permission for assignment (forms/procedure)
  • Documents of the parties: passports/ID/POA, for companies – board resolutions
  • Payments: installments by SPA, developer/admin fees, broker commission
  • Final: re-registration  of Oqood to the buyer, update of the payment schedule

9.7. Rental checklist (Ejari)

Before signing:

  • Right of ownership: Title / Oqood (handover letter if new building)
  • Passport/tenant ID; Resident status (for fixed term)
  • POA (if the owner/tenant acts through a representative)
  • Conditions: term, rate, deposit, checks/guarantees, rules (pets, short-let)
  • Property status: technical inspection, defects, meters (DEWA), parking
  • If the property is rented out, notifications to the current tenant/access for viewings

Typography:

  • RERA Unified Agreement (or equivalent) signed
  • Ejari registered (without Ejari: visa/DEWA/Etisalat/du = problems)
  • Keys/Cards/Defect List/Acceptance Certificate

After:

  • Reminders (indexation according to Decree 43/2013, eviction notices)
  • Rental folder: contract, Ejari, receipts, correspondence, acts

9.8. Examples of Wording for POAs (Fragments)

«… authorizes (full name, passport No ...) sell the  property owned by the principal: [Community/Building/Unit/Title No], sign all necessary documents and agreements on his behalf (including Form A/B/F, MOU/SPA), SUBMIT AND RECEIVE NOC FROM THE DEVELOPER, RECEIVE/ISSUE MANAGER'S CHEQUES, BE PRESENT AT THE Registration Trustee, register the transfer of rights in the DLD, draw up and terminate the lease (Ejari), liaise with DLD, RERA, the developer, banking institutions and other competent authorities of the UAE on all matters related to this transaction..."

To buy , change "sell" to "buy, sign B/F/SPA, pay and register the right, accept keys/documents".

Important: these are examples of wording. The actual text of the POA should be prepared/checked by a lawyer/notary, taking into account the specific case.

9.9. Typical mistakes and how to avoid them

  • "General" power of attorney without specifics → clarify the powers / make a Special POA with a list of actions and object.
  • No legalization/transfer → always plan 1-2 weeks to complete the chain (for overseas POAs).
  • There are no rights to "receive checks/sign F/NOC" → add these items explicitly.
  • Data mismatch (passport ↔, POA title ↔) → check the Latin alphabet, numbers, dates.
  • "First advertising – then documents" → first Contract A → Trakheesi permit, then marketing.
  • Ignoring the rental/HOA → check Ejari, deposits, notifications, service charges are part of the TCO and the risk of the deal.
  • Non-transparent payments → only Trust Account (off-plan) and manager's cheques according to the Trustee (ready) scheme.

9.10. Mini-workshop (20–30 minutes)

Task:

  1. Take a real case of a non-resident seller → make a list of powers for Special POA (sale ready), specify the object and actions.
  2. Fill in the seller's DD matrix (ready): title/mortgage/HOA/NOC/Ejari.
  3. Assemble a buyer's package (mortgage): pre-approval, KYC, checklist for F from Trustee.

Criteria: completeness of POA wording, absence of "holes" (NOC/checks/registration), correctness of DD and payment logic.
